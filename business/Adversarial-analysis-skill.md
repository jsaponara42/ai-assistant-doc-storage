# Adversarial Analysis Skill

A structured process for stress-testing any client-facing asset — offer, email, page, script, or proposal — by iterating through objections and self-refutation until the output can no longer be meaningfully challenged.

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill *before* anything goes to a prospect. Specifically:

- Cold email sequences (subject lines, body copy, CTAs)
- Offer framing (the core promise, pricing, positioning)
- Landing pages and one-pagers
- Sales call scripts and discovery questions
- Audit reports and deliverables that will be read by a decision-maker
- Any ad or social content designed to generate inbound

---

## The Process

### Step 1 — Articulate the claim

State clearly what the asset is claiming, promising, or implying. What does the reader walk away believing?

### Step 2 — Generate objections (adversarial layer)

Play the role of the most skeptical, resistant version of the ideal client. Surface every reason they would:
- Dismiss the claim as too good to be true
- Not trust the source
- Not believe it applies to them
- Feel the risk outweighs the benefit
- Prefer the status quo
- Simply not act

Do not soften these. The objections should be *hard*.

### Step 3 — Refute or absorb

For each objection, determine:
- **Refute** — the asset can be modified to directly or implicitly address this objection
- **Absorb** — the objection is valid and should be acknowledged; reframe so it doesn't undermine the offer
- **Accept** — some objections reveal a real weakness; either fix the offer or accept that this segment won't convert

### Step 4 — Revise

Produce a revised version of the asset that reflects the strongest objections addressed in Step 3. Do not add length unnecessarily — absorb objections *implicitly* where possible (through specificity, social proof, framing) rather than by adding defensive copy.

### Step 5 — Re-run (iterate)

Repeat Steps 2–4 on the revised version. Continue until:
- No new hard objections surface, or
- The objections remaining are ones the asset consciously accepts (e.g., it's not for everyone)

Typical depth: 2–3 rounds. Stop earlier if the output stabilizes quickly; go deeper if the stakes are high.

---

## Output format

Return:
1. **Final revised asset** — the output of the last clean iteration
2. **Objection log** — a brief list of the objections found and how each was handled (refuted / absorbed / accepted)
3. **Known weaknesses** — any accepted objections the asset doesn't resolve, with a note on why that's okay (or flagging it if it's not)

---

## Principles

- **Refute hard, not soft.** Weak objections produce weak improvements. Push until you'd personally hesitate to send it.
- **Specificity is armor.** Most objections dissolve when vague claims are replaced with concrete, specific ones.
- **Don't add defensiveness.** Addressing an objection by adding a caveat usually weakens the copy. Find structural solutions.
- **The goal is convergence, not perfection.** The asset is ready when further adversarial pressure produces no new meaningful objections.
