# Workflow Waste Audit — Call Flow & Questionnaire
**Blue Tusk LLC · Free 30-Minute Discovery Call**

---

## Before the Call

Skim their pre-call screening survey. Note:
- Their stated biggest pain point (Question 6)
- Tools they flagged (Question 8)
- Lead volume tier (Question 3)

Use this to skip anything they've already answered and to warm up the conversation with something specific to them.

---

## Call Structure Overview

| Phase | Time | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Intros & Rapport | 0–5 min | Build trust, let them talk |
| 2. Firm Snapshot | 5–10 min | 3 questions, orient yourself |
| 3. Quick Win Diagnostic | 10–22 min | One question per area, listen and score |
| 4. Open Pain | 22–26 min | One question, let them run |
| 5. Close & Commit | 26–30 min | Book the debrief |

---

## Phase 1 — Intros & Rapport (0–5 min)

This is real buffer time. If they're on time, use it to warm up. If they're late, you're not scrambling.

Let them lead. Ask something low-stakes and genuine:
- "How long have you been running the firm?"
- "How's the caseload been lately — busy season or more of a slow period?"

Then frame the call before moving on:

> *"Here's how I want to use our time — I'm just going to ask you a few questions about how the firm operates today and take notes. No pitch on this call. Within 72 hours I'll send you a written report showing where I think you're losing the most time and money, and what I'd fix first. Then we can look at it together on a second call. Sound good?"*

---

## Phase 2 — Firm Snapshot (5–10 min)

**Goal:** Three questions. Get enough context to make the report specific to them. Don't overdo it — most of this becomes filler in the report anyway.

**Ask:**
1. "Roughly how many new leads or inquiries are you getting per month?"
2. "What system are you using to manage cases and clients — Clio, something else, spreadsheets?"
3. "Who's handling things on the operations side — do you have staff for intake, or is it more of a shared thing?"

That's it. Move on. You'll fill in the rest from context as they talk.

---

## Phase 3 — Quick Win Diagnostic (10–22 min)

**Goal:** Score all four quick wins. You have about 3 minutes per area.

You're not interrogating them — you're getting them to describe their current process. **One open question per area is enough.** Their answer will tell you the status. If it sounds manual, it is. If they don't mention automation unprompted, it probably doesn't exist.

**Scoring guide (use mentally, don't say out loud):**
- Automated — they describe a system that runs without someone doing it manually
- Partial — something exists but a person still has to be involved at some step
- Manual/None — a person does it every time, or it just doesn't happen

Write down one quote per area in their exact words. That goes in the report.

---

### Quick Win 1 — Referral Machine

*What you're listening for: do past clients ever hear from the firm again after their case closes?*

**Ask:**
"When a case settles and the client gets their check — what happens after that? Do you stay in touch with them at all?"

**If they say yes, follow with:**
"Is that something your team does manually or does something trigger it?"

Listen for: "We mean to but..." / "We send a card sometimes" / "No, not really" / silence = gap

---

### Quick Win 2 — Case Status Automation

*What you're listening for: are clients getting updates proactively, or are they calling in to ask?*

**Ask:**
"When clients want to know what's happening with their case — how does that usually work? Are they calling you, or are you reaching out to them?"

Listen for: "They call us" / "My paralegals are on the phone all day" / "We try to update people but..." = gap

---

### Quick Win 3 — No-Show Eliminator

*What you're listening for: do booked intake calls actually happen, or do people vanish?*

**Ask:**
"When someone books an intake consultation — what's your no-show situation like? Do people actually show up?"

Listen for: "It's bad" / "Maybe half" / "We call them to remind them" / "Sarah does it when she has time" = gap

---

### Quick Win 4 — New Client Onboarding

*What you're listening for: how long does it take to go from a verbal yes to a fully open case?*

**Ask:**
"Once someone says yes to hiring you — how long does it usually take before you have everything you need to actually open the case?"

Listen for: "Days" / "A week sometimes" / "We're always waiting on forms" / "My paralegal chases them" = gap

---

## Phase 4 — Open Pain (22–26 min)

**Goal:** One question. Get them talking about their real frustration — the thing that keeps them up at night that isn't on your quick win menu. Don't interrupt. This is often the most valuable part of the call.

**Ask:**
"Outside of everything we just talked about — if you could fix one thing about how your firm runs tomorrow, what would it be?"

Let them go. Write down their exact words. This section writes itself in the report.

You're listening for: staff management issues, marketing frustration, not knowing their numbers, being stuck in the weeds personally, not having time to grow the firm.

**One natural follow-up if they stall:**
"Is there anything you find yourself doing that you know you probably shouldn't be doing at your level?"

---

## Phase 5 — Close & Commit (26–30 min)

Don't let the call end without a booked debrief. This is the only hard rule.

**Script:**
"This was really helpful — I've got a clear picture. Here's what I'll do: I'll put together a written report in the next couple of days showing exactly what I found, what it's probably costing you, and what I'd fix first. I'll send it to you by [specific day].

I'd love to walk you through it together — takes about 20 minutes. Can we find a time right now while we're both here?"

Send the Calendly link in the chat while you're still on the call. Confirm their email address. Don't hang up without a date on the calendar.
