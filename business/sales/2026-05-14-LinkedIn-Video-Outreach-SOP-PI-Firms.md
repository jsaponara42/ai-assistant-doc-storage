# SOP — LinkedIn Personalized Video Outreach + Follow-Up Flow (PI Firms)

---

## Summary

This SOP combines the firm-specific research methodology from the Workflow Waste Snapshot approach with the LinkedIn social outbound follow-up system. The entry point is a personalized LinkedIn video message referencing a specific, researched gap at the firm — not a generic pitch. The follow-up flow mirrors the social outbound sequence: voice note nudges → gif/meme follow-ups → Calendly link → Calendly'd follow-ups. No automation at any stage.

**References:**
- Cold Outreach SOP (current): [[business/SOPs/2026-04-30-SOP-Cold-Outreach-PI-Firms]]
- Social Outbound SOP (v3): [[business/sales/2026-04-30-Social_Outbound_SOP_Free_Workflow_Waste_Audit]]

---

## Why Combine These Two Methods

The email snapshot method delivers a credible, personalized first touch — but PI attorneys don't live in their inbox. LinkedIn is where managing partners are accessible, where a face and voice can carry trust faster than cold email, and where the gif/meme follow-up sequence is native to the platform's informal register.

The problem with previous LinkedIn outreach (near-zero response on ~430 contacts) was that it was generic. This method fixes that: the video is firm-specific, built on real research, and delivers a finding before asking for anything. The follow-up flow then handles persistence without tipping into spam.

---

## Capacity Constraint

Same constraint as the audit offer: **3 audits per month.** Always lead with this reason when stating the cap — "I cap at three because the audit takes real time and I'm doing it myself." This is a real constraint. It legitimizes scarcity and signals quality.

---

## Step 1 — Source Targets Manually

Same sourcing approach as the email SOP. Do not use Apollo or scraped bar directories.

- Google Maps search for PI firms in a target city — prioritize firms running Google Ads (active lead flow signal)
- State bar directories filtered by PI specialty and admission date
- Martindale-Hubbell / Avvo filtered by firm size and specialty
- LinkedIn search for PI firm managing partners in specific metro areas

**Target profile:** PI firm, 3–15 staff, actively running paid ads or generating consistent lead flow, managing partner or office manager reachable on LinkedIn.

**Volume:** Work in batches of 10–15. Do not exceed 15 simultaneous active sequences until response rate is established.

---

## Step 2 — Generate the Workflow Waste Snapshot

Use the `pi-firm-snapshot` skill (see [[business/SKILLS/pi-firm-snapshot/SKILL.md]]).

Inputs required:
- Firm name
- Website URL

The skill produces 3 specific workflow gaps with cost estimates, supported by real external research (website, Google reviews, Facebook, ad presence). 

**Quality check before recording the video:**
- All 3 gaps are grounded in actual research — no generic assumptions
- You have a specific, concrete finding to lead with in the video
- The lead gap is the single strongest or most unusual finding
- Each gap has a concrete cost estimate

**For the video, you only need Gap 1.** Hold Gaps 2 and 3 for the follow-up flow.

---

## Step 3 — Record the Personalized Video Message

Record directly in LinkedIn's native video DM tool or via Loom (paste link in the message). Keep to 45–75 seconds. Do not over-produce — natural > polished for this format.

**Video script (firm-specific):**

> Hey [NAME], hope you're good.
>
> Quick one — I run Blue Tusk. We do AI and automation for PI firms — intake, follow-up, document handling.
>
> I took a look at [FIRM NAME] before reaching out. One thing stood out: [SPECIFIC GAP FROM SNAPSHOT — one sentence, concrete, cost-referenced if natural]. That's a pattern I see a lot with firms your size and it usually has a fix.
>
> I want to send you info on a free workflow audit. Three spots this month — I cap it at three because the audit takes real time and I'm doing it myself. Three-day turnaround. No pitch on the call.
>
> Thumbs up if you want the info. That's it.

**Script notes:**
- The `[SPECIFIC GAP]` line is the only thing that changes per firm. Every other line is fixed.
- Name the gap in plain language — intake delays, unsigned retainers aging out, manual follow-up, whatever the research surfaced. Don't over-explain it.
- Do not include all 3 gaps in the video. One specific finding is stronger than three vague ones.
- If you reference a cost estimate in the video, make sure it matches the snapshot output.

**Fallback — if video is declined or unavailable:**

Send as a text DM in three sequential messages:

> Hey [NAME] — need something from you real quick. Read to the end.

> I run Blue Tusk. We do AI and automation for PI firms — intake, case management, the repetitive work that eats your team's hours. I looked at [FIRM NAME] before reaching out — [SPECIFIC GAP IN ONE SENTENCE]. That's fixable. This month I'm doing 3 free workflow audits for firms I think could benefit.

> Three days, no obligation. You'll get a prioritized report of your top workflow leaks and what each one is probably costing you. No pitch on the call. Thumbs up if you want the info.

---

## Step 4 — Initial Follow-Ups (x1, if no reply)

Wait 2–3 days. Send one voice note. Use one of these:

- "Hey [NAME], would that be alright?"
- "Hey [NAME], did that video come through okay?"
- "Hey [NAME], make sense?"

One follow-up maximum before moving to the gif sequence. Keep it shorter than the original message — this is a soft nudge, not a second pitch.

---

## Step 6 — If They Engage (Thumbs Up, Reply, or Interest Signal)

When a contact engages — any positive signal — send the Calendly message below immediately. Do not delay.

**Calendly link:** https://calendly.com/bluetuskllc/blue-tusk-workflow-audit

**Calendly message:**

> Perfect. Here's the link — 30-minute call, no pitch.
>
> The audit report is yours regardless of what we decide after.
>
> I'll ask about how your firm handles intake, follow-up, document flow, and billing. Within 3 days you'll get a prioritized report: your top workflow leaks, what each one is probably costing you, and the recommended fix for each.
>
> If there's a clear fit for a fuller engagement, we can talk about that separately — only if it makes sense for you.

## Step 8 — If They Book a Call

Follow the discovery call SOP: [[business/sales/2026-03-30-audit-call-flow]]

**Positioning note for the call:** The contact has already seen one specific gap about their firm. Open the call by acknowledging that — "I mentioned [GAP] in my message — I want to dig into how that's actually playing out for your team." This signals the audit is already underway and creates continuity with why they booked.

## What to Track

For each batch of 10–15 outreaches, record:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Firm name | |
| LinkedIn profile URL | |
| Date of initial video | |
| Video viewed? | Y / N / unknown |
| Response type | Thumbs up / reply / no reply / decline |
| Calendly link sent? | Y / N |
| Call booked? | Y / N |
| Call converted? | Y / N |
| Notes | Specific gap used in video |

**Target signal:** 2+ engagements per 10 sends. If below this after 3 batches (30–45 outreaches), return to the pipeline diagnosis document for another adversarial pass.

## What Is Not In Scope for This SOP

- Email outreach (covered separately in [[business/SOPs/2026-04-30-SOP-Cold-Outreach-PI-Firms]])
- Instantly automated sequences (paused — superseded by personalized methods)
- Post-call follow-up and retainer conversion (covered separately in sales SOPs)
- Paid advertising (not in current budget)

## Next Review

Review after the first 3 batches (30–45 outreaches) or when a meaningful response pattern emerges — whichever comes first. Key question: does the firm-specific gap in the video materially improve reply rate vs. the previous generic LinkedIn approach? If yes, the next step is refining the video script and increasing batch cadence.
