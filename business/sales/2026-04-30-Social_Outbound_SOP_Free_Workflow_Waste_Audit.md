# Social Outbound SOP — Free Workflow Waste Audit

---

## Introduction

Use this resource to store all SOPs and resources for the Social Outbound System for the Free Workflow Waste Audit offer. This is the entry-point offer for PI law firms — a free, no-commitment audit that shows exactly where their intake and case management is leaking time and money, delivered in 3 days.

**Capacity constraint:** Only 3 audits per month. This is true — we cap at 3 because the audit takes real time and is done in-house, not outsourced. Lead with this reason when stating the cap. It legitimizes the constraint and signals quality.

**On social proof:** Until a real case study with hard numbers exists, lead with mechanism specificity rather than vague outcome claims. The moment you have one real result (e.g., "cut intake follow-up time by X hours/week"), add it to the opening line of the audio/video script immediately.

**On "no pitch on the call":** This is a structural promise, not just copy. Discovery calls must not drift into closing. Deliver the audit, then follow up about next steps separately. If this promise breaks, the trust framework of the entire system breaks with it.

## What Changed from v2 (and Why)

| Element | v2 | v3 | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| Video script opening | Generic tagline — "making money and saving money with AI" | Mechanism-specific — names intake, follow-up, document handling | Vague claims create distrust; specificity does the work |
| Scarcity framing | "We've only got a few spots left" | "Three spots — I cap at three because the audit takes real time and I'm doing it myself" | Scarcity without a reason reads as a pressure tactic |
| Social proof | Implied / absent | Replaced with mechanism specificity until a real case study number exists | Vague social proof ("significantly") is worse than no social proof |
| Text script | Mentioned free, no mechanism | Names deliverable explicitly — "prioritized report of your top workflow leaks and what each one is probably costing you" | Mechanism makes the offer real before they say yes |
| Engaged follow-up count | 7 | 5 | 7+ risks spam flags and reads as desperation to senior partners |
| Calendly message — price | "$2,500–$7,500/month" stated upfront | Removed; replaced with "fuller engagement if it makes sense" | Price range at this stage creates tone/trust mismatch after a casual DM entry |
| Calendly message — trust anchor | "The audit is yours either way" — buried last | Moved to line 2 | Strongest objection-killer should be visible early, not after they've already decided |
