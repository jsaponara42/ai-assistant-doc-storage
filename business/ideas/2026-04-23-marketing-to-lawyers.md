## Summary

A conversation with a former paralegal surfaced a key insight: lawyers don't respond to ads — they respond to relationships and referrals. This note captures the strategic pivot that follows: continue cultivating the PI law vertical through in-person and referral channels while developing a parallel cold-outreach niche targeting a similarly high-value market that *is* receptive to advertising.

## Context

JC spoke with someone who had worked as a paralegal at multiple firms. She confirmed that his marketing was comparatively good, but that the pathway to lawyers goes through relationships, not ads. This prompted a rethink of the Blue Tusk go-to-market approach for the legal vertical.

## Content

### What doesn't work (for lawyers)
- Cold email / ad-based outreach — lawyers are not receptive
- General awareness campaigns — insufficient to generate sales calls

### What does work (for lawyers)
- In-person relationship building at industry events
- Referral networks — once a few clients are on board, word-of-mouth takes over
- Working through office managers as an entry point (worth exploring)
