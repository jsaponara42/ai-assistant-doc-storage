## Summary

Ideas for improving the social outbound SOP and cold email sequence for the Free Workflow Waste Audit. Focused on scarcity, benefit clarity, consistency messaging, and more direct CTAs.

## Context

## Content

### Scarcity / Time Limit
- Add a capacity constraint to the audit offer: "I'm only doing 3 this month."
- Creates urgency without manufactured pressure. Positions the audit as limited-access, not a blanket free offer.

### Benefit Clarity — Be Blunt
- Lead more directly with the core outcome: **making money and saving money**.
- Suggested framing: "Making money. Saving money. With AI and automation in your firm."
- Don't bury the value in mechanism. State the outcome first, then explain how.

### Expense Signal
- Mention that the full service can get expensive — frames the free audit as genuinely valuable, not cheap.
- Reduces the "what's the catch" skepticism by contextualizing the cost of the paid engagement.

### Consistency Messaging
- How does agreeing to an audit keep the prospect consistent with what they already do and value in their firm?
- Use the principle of consistency: they already invest in their team, already care about efficiency. The audit is the next logical step.

### Use "Because"
- Structure CTAs with "because" to complete the cognitive loop:
  - "I'd like you to book a meeting *because* most PI firms find $20–70k they didn't know they were losing."
  - Forces specificity and makes the ask feel grounded.

### Direct / Attention-First Variant
- Try a more assertive opener: "Need something from you" / "Need your attention" / "Let's find some time to talk"
- Follow immediately with a "because" to justify the directness.
- Tests a different persona: confident and clear vs. warm and peer-to-peer.
