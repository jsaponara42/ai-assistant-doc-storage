# Writing Style Guide — Blue Tusk

This guide applies to all copy written for Blue Tusk LLC. Read it before writing client facing, or marketing text.

---

## The Core Principle

Write like a specific person, not like a summary. Prefer concrete and direct language over language that sounds balanced, comprehensive, or formal.

The test: would John-Carlos say this exact sentence to someone he met at a conference? If it would sound rehearsed or pitched out loud, rewrite it.

---

## Grammar and Construction Rules

### Use plain copulas
Prefer "is" and "are" over:
- "serves as"
- "stands as"
- "marks"
- "represents"
- "boasts"
- "features"
- "offers"

When a simple verb works, use it.

**Wrong:** "The audit serves as a diagnostic tool for PI firms."
**Right:** "The audit shows PI firms where their time is going."

---

### No negative parallelisms
Do not write constructions like:
- "not only X, but Y"
- "it's not X, it's Y"
- "not X — but Y"

Say what the thing is. Do not pre-empt imaginary misconceptions.

**Wrong:** "It's not a follow-up problem. It's a revenue problem."
**Right:** "Slow response loses cases — specifically the best ones."

*(Note: the "it's not X, it's Y" construction appeared in earlier sequence drafts and was effective in that context. Use judgment — this rule is about avoiding the pattern as a crutch, not banning contrast entirely.)*

---

### No rule of three
Do not default to listing things in threes (adjective, adjective, adjective / phrase, phrase, and phrase). Use one strong word instead of three weak ones.

**Wrong:** "Your intake is inconsistent, manual, and unpredictable."
**Right:** "Your intake depends on whoever is working that day."

---

### No elegant variation
If you've already named the subject, use the same word again. Do not rotate synonyms to avoid repetition.

**Wrong:** "The audit... the report... the assessment... the diagnostic..."
**Right:** "The audit... the audit..."

Synonym rotation is a clear AI signal. Repetition of the right word reads as confident.

---

### No em-dashes
Em-dashes have become an AI writing tell. Replace every em-dash with either:
- A period and a new sentence
- A comma

**Wrong:** "I keep my threads open — reply whenever it makes sense."
**Right:** "I keep my threads open. Reply whenever it makes sense."

## Voice and Tone

**This is the vibe:** A well-connected business owner reaching out to a peer because he genuinely thinks he can help. High status, casual, not trying to prove anything. Generous with what he knows. Not desperate.

- Short sentences over long ones
- Questions over statements when possible
- Specific over general — name the thing, give the number, describe the scenario
- No corporate phrasing — write the way someone would text a contact they respect
- Signatures get shorter as the sequence progresses (full name + title on Email 1, first name only by Email 4)
