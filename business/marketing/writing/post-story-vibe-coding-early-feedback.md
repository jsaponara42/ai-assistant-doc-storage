## Draft

One of the most underrated things about building with AI is how early you can get real feedback.

Before, getting input on a product or tool meant one of two things. You either described it, which meant people were reacting to an idea, not a thing. Or you waited until something was built, which meant feedback came too late to change the fundamentals.

The middle option — a wireframe, a mockup, a prototype — helped, but it still required significant time and skill before stakeholders could see something that felt real.

That gap is mostly gone now.

With AI, you can have something interactive in front of people in hours. Not a description. Not a sketch. Something they can actually use. And when people can use something, they give you a completely different quality of feedback. They stop reacting to what they imagined and start reacting to what's actually there.

That means fewer surprises late in the build. It means changes happen when they're cheap instead of when they're not. And it means the people who will actually use the thing have a say in it before you've committed to anything.

The speed of building gets most of the attention. The quality of early feedback is the part that actually changes the outcome.
