## Draft

I got caught by a hallucination recently. And I use AI every day.

A friend and I were talking about politics. We got into a discussion about presidential history and I wanted to add some context. I asked Claude a question off the top of my head: how many times has a president from a different party than their predecessor won the White House, not counting someone who took over through death or resignation?

It told me it had never happened.

That is very wrong. It has happened many times. But in the moment, I just went with it. The conversation moved in a direction based on a completely false premise and neither of us caught it until later.

Here's what stuck with me. Before AI, if I didn't know something I'd say I didn't know. Or I'd look it up and have a reasonable sense of whether the source was reliable. There was uncertainty built into the interaction.

AI removes that uncertainty from the surface. The answer comes back confident, clean, and formatted. And if you're not in the habit of questioning it, that confidence transfers to you. You repeat it. You build on it. The conversation goes somewhere it shouldn't.

I'm not saying don't use AI. I'm saying the risk isn't that AI gives you wrong answers. It's that it gives you wrong answers in a way that makes you feel right.

That's a different problem than being uninformed. And it's worth taking seriously.
