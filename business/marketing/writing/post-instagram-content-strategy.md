## Final

Editing is the part of content creation that kills the schedule.

Not filming. Not coming up with ideas. The actual sit-down-and-edit part. That's where time goes.

So I changed the filming process. Instead of recording complete videos one at a time, I now film in three separate passes: hooks, middles, and call to actions. Each one is its own batch. Film 20 pieces total and you have roughly 7 of each type.

Then I built a tool with AI that stitches them together. Every combination of hook, middle, and CTA becomes a finished video. Seven hooks times seven middles times seven CTAs is a few hundred variations. All of them get dropped into an output folder, ready to post or schedule.

Nothing off the shelf does this. There's no app that lets you define beginning, middle, and end as separate buckets and then generate every combination. So I built one. AI helped me write the code. I pointed it at the right folders. It handles the stitching.

The editing bottleneck is gone. The content calendar fills up from one filming session.

This is the kind of thing I mean when I talk about boring AI. It's not revolutionary. It's one repetitive task that used to take hours now taking minutes. That's it.
