# SOP — Warm and Cold Reconnection Outreach

## Summary

This SOP governs how John-Carlos reaches out to both old connections (warm) and cold contacts for the purpose of customer discovery, relationship building, and eventual consulting pipeline development. The approach is rooted in Cialdini's reciprocation principle: offer something genuinely valuable first, create goodwill, and create the conditions for a future upsell. This is the first documented version of this process.

## Current Process

### Part A — Warm Outreach (Old Connections)

Use this sequence for people JC has had at least one real prior interaction with — past advisors, collaborators, event contacts, fellow speakers, etc.

**Goal:** Re-establish presence, show genuine interest in their world, create natural space to share what you're working on and invite suggestions or introductions.

1. Before writing, recall or look up one specific, concrete thing about them — a project they mentioned, a company move, something they were building. This is the anchor for the opening line.

2. Choose your channel based on whether you have their number:

   - **Have their number → Start with SMS** (step 2a), then email if no reply
   - **No number → Start with email** (step 2b)

   **2a. SMS — The Casual Ping:**

   > Hey [First name]! [One sentence referencing something specific — e.g., "Was just thinking about that waste facility you were building — did that ever get off the ground?"] Hope you're doing well man.

   Keep it to 2–3 sentences max. Conversational, no punctuation pressure, no signature. This should read like a text from a normal person, not a business contact. No ask, no pitch.

   Wait 3–5 days. If they reply, great — pick up the conversation naturally and work toward the reciprocal share (step 4). If no reply, move to email (step 2b).

   **2b. Email 1 — The Re-engagement Ping:**

   > Subject: [First name] — checking in
   >
   > Hey [First name]!
   >
   > It's been [time period] since [specific thing you last discussed or learned about them — e.g., "you mentioned you were planning to roll up a couple of waste companies on the east coast"]. Are you still pursuing that / How did that turn out?
   >
   > [One natural sentence connecting why they came to mind — a shared experience, a recent event, something relevant.]
   >
   > Best,
   > John-Carlos

   Keep this email short. Three sentences max in the body. No ask. No pitch.

3. Wait for reply. If they reply, move to step 4. If no reply after 7 days, send one follow-up:

   > Subject: Re: [First name] — checking in
   >
   > Hey [First name] — just bumping this up in case it got buried. No rush at all, hope things are going well.
   >
   > Best,
   > John-Carlos

   If still no reply after another 7 days, mark as dormant and move on. Do not push further.

4. Reply to their response — The Reciprocal Share:

   Respond to what they shared. Acknowledge it specifically. Then share a relevant update of your own — keep it honest, including the rough parts (low trust markets, pivoting, figuring things out). This signals you're a real person, not a pitch machine.

   End with a soft, open-ended offer:

   > I'm looking to do some free AI and automation consultations in [relevant industry / "a few different industries"] to feel out where the need is — open to any suggestions you might have.

   Or if more context is appropriate:

   > I'm doing some free discovery conversations with operators in different industries — happy to share what I'm seeing if it's useful for what you're building.

   This is the reciprocation trigger: you're offering value, not asking for a sale. Deliver this via whatever channel the conversation is already happening in — SMS if it started there, email if not.

5. If they express interest or refer you to someone, move to the discovery call process (not in scope here — see below).

6. Log the contact in your tracking sheet (see "What to Track").

### Part B — Cold Outreach

Use this sequence for people JC has no prior relationship with. The frame is: you want to learn from them, and you'll give them something useful in return.

**Goal:** Earn a short discovery conversation by leading with curiosity and a concrete free offer — not a pitch.

1. Identify the target. Know their industry, their role, and one specific thing about what they do or build. Do not send a generic email.

2. Send Email 1 — The Curiosity Open:

   > Subject: Quick question from a fellow [operator / founder / builder] — [First name]
   >
   > Hey [First name],
   >
   > I'm [JC name and one-line context — e.g., "a consultant working at the intersection of AI and small business operations"]. I came across your work at [company / context] and I'm doing some research into how [their industry] is approaching [relevant operational challenge — e.g., intake, scheduling, case management, client communication].
   >
   > I'd love to ask you a few questions — 20 minutes, no pitch, no agenda. In return, I'll share what I'm seeing across the operators I'm talking to, and if I spot anything useful for your setup specifically, I'll share that too.
   >
   > Worth a quick call?
   >
   > Best,
   > John-Carlos Saponara
   > Founder — Blue Tusk LLC
   > bluetuskllc.com

   Keep it under 100 words in the body. The offer of reciprocal value is explicit and in the email — this is intentional.

3. Wait 5–7 business days. If no reply, send one follow-up only:

   > Subject: Re: Quick question — [First name]
   >
   > Hey [First name] — just following up on this in case it got buried. Happy to keep it very short — even a 15-minute call would be helpful. And if it's not a fit, totally understand.
   >
   > Best,
   > John-Carlos

4. If they reply and agree to a call, confirm the time and prepare 5–7 open-ended discovery questions specific to their industry before the call.

5. On the call: ask questions, listen, take notes. At the end, deliver the free value you promised — share what you're observing across operators, or offer a quick observation specific to their setup. Do not pitch on the first call.

6. After the call, send a brief follow-up thank-you within 24 hours:

   > Hey [First name],
   >
   > Really appreciated you taking the time — learned a lot. [One specific thing you found interesting or useful from the conversation.]
   >
   > I'll keep you posted on what I find as I talk to more operators. And if anything comes up where I can be useful to you, I'm happy to help.
   >
   > Best,
   > John-Carlos

7. Log the contact and call notes (see "What to Track"). If there's a clear upsell opportunity, wait at least one week before any follow-up that involves an offer.

## What to Track

For every contact (warm or cold), log the following:

- **Name and company**
- **Source:** warm or cold
- **Industry / vertical**
- **Channel used:** SMS / email
- **Date of first outreach**
- **Reply received:** yes / no
- **Call completed:** yes / no / not applicable
- **Key insight from conversation** (1–2 sentences)
- **Upsell signal:** strong / weak / none
- **Next action and date**

A "success" at this stage is a completed call with notes. An upsell signal is any moment where they described a pain point they'd pay to solve. A review is triggered if reply rate drops below 20% across 10+ cold contacts — adjust the email copy or targeting.
