# Call Brief — NeighborWorks Capital
**Date:** ___________
**Industry:** CDFI / community development finance (nonprofit lender)

---

## The Company
- **What they do:** National nonprofit CDFI providing flexible, patient loans + consulting to the ~250-member NeighborWorks America network. $600M+ deployed.
- **Size:** ~24–26 staff (16–50 tier). HQ Silver Spring, MD.
- **Stage:** Established (founded 2000) but in an active growth + modernization phase — heavy 2024–2026 senior hiring.
- **Tech signals:** Salesforce (borrower portal + active expansion as service/insight platform), WordPress site, dedicated tech-ops role. Mid-maturity, actively building.
- **Notable:** AERIS 4-star rated. New CEO (2022) + 4 new C-suite since 2023. Dedicated Salesforce-expansion project underway (Brendan Barry). Manual portfolio reporting is a named function.

---

## The People

### Tiana Coll — Director, Analysis & Technology Operations (primary)
- **Tenure:** At NWC since 2021; 20 yrs total (15 at Enterprise Community Partners).
- **Background:** Program oversight + technology systems for community development; economics degree. Owns reporting and the tech stack.
- **Frame:** Operator / system-owner — the actual decision influencer on tooling. Speaks data + infrastructure, not budget politics.
- **Notable:** Explicitly responsible for portfolio analysis, reporting, tech infrastructure, vendor management, and cybersecurity. This is your champion if there's a fit.

### Rachel Haywood — Office Manager (secondary)
- **Tenure:** At NWC since Nov 2021; 7+ yrs prior at a commercial real estate firm.
- **Background:** Day-to-day office operations + marketing; CRE and retail merchandising ops before NWC.
- **Frame:** Operator / daily-friction owner — feels the manual admin pain firsthand. Ground-truth on what actually eats hours.
- **Notable:** Pairing tech-owner + office-manager on one call is ideal — strategy and lived workflow in the same room.

---

## Anticipated Pains
1. **Manual portfolio & investor reporting.** Loan/portfolio data assembled by hand for the loan committee, board, investors, and CDFI Fund compliance — recurring, deadline-driven, error-prone.
2. **Borrower data collection bottleneck.** Chasing borrowers for current financials and loan info through the portal — follow-up is manual, and stale data stalls reporting and monitoring.
3. **Loan closing & draw-processing admin.** Document-heavy closings and construction/predevelopment draw requests handled largely by hand across a small team.
4. **Salesforce expansion outpacing capacity.** Actively expanding Salesforce but with one project manager — integration, automation, and adoption likely lag the ambition.
5. **CDFI compliance & documentation burden.** CDFI Fund, AERIS rating, and investor covenants demand structured, repeatable documentation — heavy for a ~25-person shop.

---

## Sharp Questions to Ask
- "Walk me through how a portfolio or investor report comes together today — who touches it, and how many hours does one cycle take?"
- "When a borrower's financials are due, how does that follow-up actually happen — and how often does missing data hold up your reporting?"
- "Where are you in the Salesforce expansion, and what's the gap between what you want it to do and what it does today?"
- "What's taking the most time in your operation right now that you wish wasn't?"
- "What have you already tried to fix the manual reporting side, and where did it fall short?"
- "Rachel — what eats the most of your day that feels like it shouldn't be a person's job?"

---

## What to Listen For
Tiana naming a specific recurring report or data-pull that burns real hours, or describing the Salesforce build as under-resourced, is a strong fit. If she frames the problem as "we know what we want, we just can't get there with current capacity," that's a ready-to-move signal. A concrete, time-quantified pain (not a vague "we could be more efficient") means move to a discovery session.

---

## Red Flags
Neither attendee is C-suite, so budget authority isn't in the room — confirm who signs off before scoping anything paid. Watch for "we just hired people / bought Salesforce to handle this" — they may feel the problem is already being solved internally. As a CDFI on grants + investor capital, procurement can be slow and cautious; expect a longer path than a for-profit of the same size.
