# Maycomb Capital — Legal Bills Process

**April 2026**

**Purpose:** The purpose of this document is to provide a standardized process for tracking, allocating, and paying legal bills across the firm.

**Note:** Legal bills are initially paid by the Management Company through the bi-weekly AP run process. This document defines when and how these costs are allocated and reimbursed to the Management Company.

## Process

### 1. Legal Work Performed
- Legal services are performed by external counsel.

### 2. Invoice Receipt and Storage
- Legal bills are sent to Maycomb from lawyers (e.g. Manatt, Morgan Lewis, etc).
- If legal bills are sent directly to the Deal Team, they should be forwarded to the Operations Team.
- Operations Team saves down legal bill into the Legal Bill folder (specified for law firm).

### 3. Invoice Tracking
- Operations Team inputs legal bill details into the [Legal Bills Tracker](https://maycombcapital.sharepoint.com/:x:/s/FinanceOperations/IQBF3AM09JJ2SII4AnsgHr9OATwE8wEvgRYAWrh-kQ46MEM?e=7kBxwk) excel.
- Including invoice number, date, amount, allocation, payment status, description, etc.

### 4. Responsibility and Allocation Determination
- On a monthly basis, the Operations Team (in coordination with Deal Teams) gathers all legal invoices accumulated in that time and determine:
  - Which entity has **responsibility** for the cost of the bill, and
  - Which entity the cost will be **allocated** to.
- Once the **responsibility** and **allocation** are determined by the Operations team, they need to be **approved**.
  - If the decision regarding the responsibility and allocation of the bill is straightforward, Operations team approval is sufficient.
  - If the bill is being charged to a Fund and the decision is more discretionary (ex. a modified loan template), the Operations team will send a monthly list of allocations for Partnership Expenses legal bills to Andi for her review.
- After granted **approval**, relevant payment instructions are followed for each bill (see details below).

There are three primary scenarios for **responsibility** and **allocation**:

#### Management Company Responsibility and Allocation
- **Use when:** Legal work relates to Management Company activities (ex. internal policies, HR matters, etc.)
- **Payment Instructions:**
  - Paid by the Management Company via AP run
  - No further allocation required
- **Note:** If the Management Company elects to cover the cost of a bill that would otherwise belong to a Fund or a Borrower, document this decision in the Legal Bills Tracker.

#### Fund Responsibility and Allocation
- **Use when:** Legal work relates to Fund-level matters (ex. subscription documents, side letters, etc)
- **Payment Instructions:**
  - Paid by the Management Company via AP run
  - Marked as due from Fund.
- **Note:** If Fund agrees to cover Borrower-related legal costs (ex. Borrower exceeds legal limit), document this in Legal Bills Tracker.

#### Deal-level Responsibility, Fund Allocation
- **Use when:** Legal work relates to a deal and the size of the bill remains within the legal cost limit, where applicable (ex. loan agreement/amendment, etc)
- **Payment Instructions:**
  - Either:
    - Deduct legal bill amount from a loan disbursement, or
    - Invoice Borrower (payment for invoice directed to the Fund)
  - After reimbursement is confirmed from either method above:
    - Management Company pays invoice via the AP run
    - Marked as due from Fund
- **Note:** The legal bill will **NOT** be paid by the Management Company until the cash to cover the bill is in the Fund account, either as a result of a deduction from a loan advance or paid from an invoice to the Borrower.

### 5. Payment and Status Update
- Once payment is complete, update the Legal Bills Tracker (payment and allocation columns).
- On a quarterly basis before the Due to Manager balances are paid, all legal invoices included are reviewed by the Operations team to ensure proper allocation.
