# Maycomb Capital — Annual Budget & Budget-to-Actuals Process

**Standard Operating Procedure**

## 1. Purpose

This SOP documents Maycomb Capital's annual budgeting process and how the budget is monitored and updated throughout the year. It covers how the initial annual budget is built, how it is reviewed against actual spending each month, and how it is formally right-sized in June and December to reflect real business conditions.

This document is written for someone stepping into the Finance & Operations Associate role with a general understanding of business finance, but no prior knowledge of Maycomb Capital's specific systems or workflows.

## 2. Background & Key Concepts

**What is a Budget?**
A budget is a forward-looking plan for how much money the business expects to spend in each category over the course of the year. It is built at the start of the year based on prior year spending and known upcoming needs, and it serves as a benchmark throughout the year.

**What is Budget-to-Actuals?**
Budget-to-actuals (also called 'budget vs. actuals') is a comparison of what the business planned to spend (the budget) versus what it actually spent (the actuals). This comparison is prepared monthly by Ultimus Leverpoint and included in the monthly financial statements. It helps identify areas where the business is overspending or underspending relative to plan.

**What is Right-Sizing?**
Right-sizing means updating the budget mid-year to reflect reality. If actual spending in a category is consistently higher or lower than originally budgeted, or if a significant new expense has emerged (e.g., a new software tool), the budget is revised so that projections for the rest of the year remain accurate.

**Why Does the December Review Matter?**
Maycomb Capital needs a close-to-finalized view of full-year financials in December in order to calculate and pay quarterly tax estimates before year-end. This makes the December budget review time-sensitive and important.

**What is Ultimus Leverpoint's Role Here?**
Ultimus Leverpoint (the fund administrator) tracks Maycomb Capital's actual spending throughout the year and produces monthly financial statements, which include a budget-to-actuals comparison page. They use the budget that Maycomb provides at the start of the year (and any updates sent during the year) to build this comparison. They do not build the budget — that is done internally.

## 5. Annual Calendar

| When | Activity | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| January | Annual budget build | Finance & Ops Associate and COO build the full-year budget in Excel. Andi approves via Slack. Sent to Ultimus by email. |
| February–May | Monthly monitoring | Review monthly financials from Ultimus (emailed as attachments). Note any significant variances in the budget-to-actuals page. No formal revision unless a major change occurs. |
| June | Mid-year right-sizing | Deep-dive review of budget-to-actuals. Revise budget for remainder of year. Andi approves via Slack. Updated budget sent to Ultimus by email. |
| July–November | Monthly monitoring | Continue reviewing monthly financials. Incorporate any significant unplanned expenses (e.g., new software tools). Send ad-hoc updates to Ultimus as needed. |
| December | Year-end right-sizing | Final budget revision to project accurate year-end financials. Critical for tax estimate calculations. Andi approves via Slack. Updated budget sent to Ultimus by email. |
| Year-end | Finalization | Once the year closes, Ultimus produces final financials. These become the actuals baseline for the following year's budget. |

## 6. Step-by-Step Process

The budgeting process has four recurring phases: the initial build, monthly monitoring, and two formal revisions. Each is documented below.

### Step 1: Build the Annual Budget (January)
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | (1) Prior year actuals (from Ultimus's final year-end financial statements). (2) Knowledge of upcoming business activities for the new year: planned hires, events (e.g., annual general meeting, team retreat), software changes, contractor updates, pricing changes for existing vendors. |
| **Where inputs come from** | Prior year financials: saved on SharePoint (named: [Month] [Year] MOutcomes FS). Upcoming business activities: discussed between the Finance & Operations Associate and the COO. |
| **Software & tools** | Microsoft Excel (SharePoint); Slack (for Andi's approval); Email (Outlook, to send to Ultimus) |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate and COO (build together); Managing Partner Andi (approves); Finance & Operations Associate (sends to Ultimus) |
| **Outputs** | Completed annual budget Excel file, approved by Andi and sent to Ultimus Leverpoint. |
| **Where outputs go** | Saved to SharePoint as: FY[Year] Budget Maycomb Outcomes (e.g., FY2025 Budget Maycomb Outcomes). Emailed to Ultimus Leverpoint. |
| **Notes** | The budget should include a line item for every significant expense category (compensation, legal, rent, software, events, contractors, etc.). Use prior year actuals as the starting point, then adjust upward or downward based on known changes for the new year. Once the draft is complete, share with Andi via Slack and request her approval. Do not send to Ultimus until Andi has confirmed approval in Slack. |

### Step 2: Review Monthly Financials & Monitor Budget-to-Actuals (Ongoing)
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | Monthly financial statements from Ultimus Leverpoint, which include a budget-to-actuals comparison page. |
| **Where inputs come from** | Received by email from Ultimus Leverpoint as an attachment. Save each file to SharePoint immediately upon receipt. |
| **Software & tools** | Microsoft Excel (SharePoint); Email (Outlook) |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate (primary reviewer); COO (consulted on significant variances) |
| **Outputs** | Internal notes on any significant variances or emerging budget concerns. Ad-hoc budget update sent to Ultimus if a major unplanned expense occurs outside of the June or December review cycles. |
| **Where outputs go** | Notes kept internally (can be added directly to the saved financial statement file or tracked separately). Any ad-hoc budget updates emailed to Ultimus. |
| **Notes** | Save each monthly financial statement to SharePoint as: [Month] [Year] MOutcomes FS (e.g., April 2025 MOutcomes FS). Review the budget-to-actuals page in full each month. Pay particular attention to larger spend categories such as compensation, legal, and any category where spending appears to be tracking significantly above or below budget. If a major unplanned expense arises mid-month (e.g., a new software subscription, an unplanned contractor), flag it to the COO and consider whether an ad-hoc budget update to Ultimus is needed rather than waiting for the June or December review. |

### Step 3: Mid-Year Budget Right-Sizing (June)
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | (1) Year-to-date monthly financial statements (January–May/June). (2) Current annual budget (FY[Year] Budget Maycomb Outcomes on SharePoint). (3) Updated knowledge of expected business activities for the remainder of the year. |
| **Where inputs come from** | Monthly financials saved on SharePoint. Current budget on SharePoint. Business activity updates from COO. |
| **Software & tools** | Microsoft Excel (SharePoint); Slack (for Andi's approval); Email (Outlook, to send to Ultimus) |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate and COO (build revision together); Managing Partner Andi (approves); Finance & Operations Associate (sends to Ultimus) |
| **Outputs** | Revised budget for the remainder of the year, approved by Andi and sent to Ultimus Leverpoint. |
| **Where outputs go** | Save updated budget to SharePoint (consider versioning, e.g., FY2025 Budget Maycomb Outcomes v2 or note the revision date in the filename). Email updated budget to Ultimus Leverpoint. |
| **Notes** | Conduct a thorough line-by-line review of the budget-to-actuals for the year so far. For each category, assess: (1) Is actual spending tracking above or below budget? (2) Is the variance expected to continue, or was it a one-time event? (3) Are there known upcoming expenses not reflected in the original budget? Revise projections for all remaining months accordingly. Share the updated budget with Andi via Slack for approval before sending to Ultimus. |

### Step 4: Year-End Budget Right-Sizing (December)
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | (1) Year-to-date monthly financial statements (January–November). (2) Most recent budget version on SharePoint. (3) Updated knowledge of any remaining year-end expenses. |
| **Where inputs come from** | Monthly financials saved on SharePoint. Current budget on SharePoint. Business activity updates from COO. |
| **Software & tools** | Microsoft Excel (SharePoint); Slack (for Andi's approval); Email (Outlook, to send to Ultimus) |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate and COO (build revision together); Managing Partner Andi (approves); Finance & Operations Associate (sends to Ultimus) |
| **Outputs** | Final revised budget projecting accurate year-end financials, approved by Andi and sent to Ultimus Leverpoint. |
| **Where outputs go** | Save to SharePoint (versioned or dated). Email to Ultimus Leverpoint. |
| **Notes** | This review is time-sensitive: Maycomb Capital needs a close-to-final view of year-end financials in order to calculate and pay tax estimates before December 31. Prioritize completing this review early enough in December to allow time for Andi's approval and Ultimus's incorporation of the update. Follow the same review process as Step 3 — line-by-line comparison, assess variances, project remaining spend through year-end. Once the year closes, Ultimus will produce finalized year-end financials, which become the actuals baseline for the following year's January budget build. |

## 8. Exception Handling

**Major Unplanned Expense Arises Mid-Year**
If a significant unplanned expense occurs outside of the June or December review cycles (e.g., a new CRM tool, an unplanned hire), assess with the COO whether an ad-hoc budget update should be sent to Ultimus immediately rather than waiting for the next scheduled review. If the expense is large enough to materially affect year-end projections, send an update promptly.

**Monthly Financials Are Late or Missing**
If Ultimus does not send the monthly financials within a reasonable time after month-end, follow up with Ultimus by email. Do not skip the monthly review — it is the primary mechanism for catching budget variances early.

**Significant Variance with No Clear Explanation**
If the budget-to-actuals shows a large variance in a category and the reason is not immediately clear, discuss with the COO before flagging to Ultimus. It may be a timing difference, a miscategorization, or a genuine overspend — understanding the cause determines the right response.

**Andi Is Unavailable for Approval**
The budget (and any revisions) must be approved by Andi or another Managing Partner before being sent to Ultimus. Do not send an unapproved budget. If Andi is unavailable and the timing is urgent (particularly in December), escalate to another partner.
