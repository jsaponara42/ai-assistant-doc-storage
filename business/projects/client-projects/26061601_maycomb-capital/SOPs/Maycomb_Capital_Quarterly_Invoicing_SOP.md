## 1. Purpose

This SOP documents the end-to-end process for preparing, issuing, and tracking quarterly interest invoices sent to Maycomb Capital's borrowers. It covers how the borrower list is confirmed, how invoices are created and verified, how they are sent, and how incoming payments are tracked.

This document is written for someone stepping into the Finance & Operations Associate role with a general understanding of business finance, but no prior knowledge of Maycomb Capital's specific systems or workflows.

## 2. Background & Key Concepts

**What is a Borrower Invoice?**
Maycomb Capital is a private credit firm, meaning it lends money to companies (borrowers). Each quarter, borrowers owe interest on their loans — and sometimes principal repayments or fees as well. The Finance & Operations team sends formal invoices to each borrower notifying them of what they owe and when it is due.

**When Are Invoices Sent?**
Invoices are sent 30 days before the payment due date. For most borrowers, the payment due date is the last day of the quarter (March 31, June 30, September 30, or December 31). This means invoices are sent approximately on March 1, June 1, September 1, and December 1 each year.

**What is the Loan Servicing Model?**
The loan servicing model is an Excel workbook stored on SharePoint that tracks all loan details for each borrower, including interest calculations, payment schedules, and payment history. It is the source of truth for how much each borrower owes in a given quarter.

**What are the Deal Teams?**
Deal teams are internal Maycomb Capital staff (typically investment professionals) who manage relationships with specific borrowers and funds. They have detailed knowledge of each loan's terms, including whether a borrower's interest is being capitalized, waived, or modified in a given quarter. They are the final authority on whether a borrower needs an invoice and whether the invoice details are correct.

**What Does 'Capitalizing Interest' Mean?**
Capitalizing interest means that instead of the borrower paying interest in cash this quarter, the interest amount is added to the outstanding loan balance. In this case, no invoice is sent because no cash payment is due.

**What Are the Invoice Templates?**
Each fund has its own invoice template, stored on SharePoint. The templates are pre-formatted and include the fund name and relevant details. The Finance & Operations Associate fills in borrower-specific information (contact details, amounts owed) for each invoice.

| Role | Responsibility in This Process |
| --- | --- |

## 4. Systems & Tools Used

| Tool | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| SharePoint (Maycomb Capital) | Stores the loan servicing model, borrower list, and fund-specific invoice templates. All working files live here. |
| Microsoft Excel | Used to build and update the borrower list, reference the loan servicing model for interest calculations, and complete invoice templates. |
| Slack | Used to communicate with deal teams for both the borrower list confirmation and the draft invoice review. |

## 5. Quarterly Schedule

| Timing | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Midway through the quarter | Create borrower list and send to deal teams for confirmation (Step 1). |
| 1–2 weeks before invoice send date | Create all draft invoices using loan servicing model and templates (Step 2). Send drafts to deal teams for review (Step 3). |
| ~30 days before quarter-end (invoice send date) | Send finalized invoices to borrowers via email (Step 4). |
| Quarter-end and the week following | Monitor Wells Fargo for incoming payments and update the loan servicing model (Step 5). |

## 6. Step-by-Step Process

The quarterly invoicing process is broken into 5 steps. Each step includes what you need, where to find it, what systems you'll use, who is responsible, and what you produce.

### Step 1: Create Borrower List & Confirm with Deal Teams
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | Full list of active borrowers across all funds; knowledge of any known exceptions (interest capitalizations, waivers, etc.) from prior quarters. |
| **Where inputs come from** | Loan servicing model on SharePoint (for the full borrower list). Deal teams (via Slack) for exception guidance. |
| **Software & tools** | Microsoft Excel (SharePoint); Slack |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate |
| **Outputs** | A confirmed borrower list showing which borrowers will and will not receive an invoice this quarter, with reasons noted for any exclusions. |
| **Where outputs go** | Sent to deal teams via Slack for confirmation. Saved to SharePoint. Used in Step 2 to create invoices. |
| **Notes** | Most borrowers will need an invoice. The exceptions are borrowers whose interest is being capitalized, whose payment has been waived, or who have other special arrangements for the quarter. The deal teams have final authority on these decisions — do not exclude a borrower without their written confirmation via Slack. Once deal teams confirm the list, save the final version to SharePoint before proceeding. |

### Step 2: Create Draft Invoices for Each Borrower
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | (1) Confirmed borrower list from Step 1. (2) Loan servicing model (for interest calculations). (3) Fund-specific invoice templates. (4) Borrower contact information (provided by deal teams at deal closing). |
| **Where inputs come from** | All files on SharePoint. Borrower contact info should be on file from deal closing documentation — if missing, request from the relevant deal team via Slack. |
| **Software & tools** | Microsoft Excel (SharePoint); invoice templates (SharePoint) |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate |
| **Outputs** | A completed draft invoice for each borrower on the confirmed list, saved to SharePoint using the naming convention: [Fund Name] Invoice – [Borrower Name]. |
| **Where outputs go** | Saved to SharePoint. Sent to deal teams for review in Step 3. |
| **Notes** | For each invoice: (1) Open the correct fund's invoice template from SharePoint. (2) Fill in borrower contact details (name, address, email). (3) Reference the loan servicing model to calculate the interest amount due. (4) Add any principal repayment or fee amounts — these may also be in the loan servicing model or may need to be confirmed with the deal team on a case-by-case basis. (5) Save the completed invoice as: [Fund Name] Invoice – [Borrower Name]. Double-check all amounts before sending for review. |

### Step 3: Send Draft Invoices to Deal Teams for Review
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | Completed draft invoices for all borrowers (from Step 2). |
| **Where inputs come from** | SharePoint (saved invoice files). |
| **Software & tools** | Slack |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate (sends drafts); Deal Teams (review and confirm) |
| **Outputs** | Deal team confirmation via Slack that all invoice details are correct — or a list of corrections to be made. |
| **Where outputs go** | Once all invoices are confirmed correct, proceed to Step 4. If corrections are needed, update the relevant invoices in SharePoint and re-confirm with the deal team before sending. |
| **Notes** | Share all draft invoices with the relevant deal teams via Slack. Ask them to verify: borrower contact information (name, address, email), interest amount, any principal or fee amounts, and anything else included in the invoice. Do not send invoices to borrowers until all deal teams have confirmed in writing (via Slack) that the details are correct. If a deal team requests a change, update the invoice, save it to SharePoint, and ask them to re-confirm. |

### Step 4: Send Finalized Invoices to Borrowers
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | Confirmed, finalized invoices for all borrowers (from Step 3). Borrower contact email addresses. |
| **Where inputs come from** | SharePoint (invoice files). Borrower contact information from deal closing documentation on file. |
| **Software & tools** | Email (Outlook) |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate |
| **Outputs** | Invoices emailed to each borrower contact, sent approximately 30 days before the quarter-end payment due date. |
| **Where outputs go** | Sent directly to borrower email contacts. Retain sent email records in Outlook for reference. |
| **Notes** | Send each invoice to the correct borrower contact email. The send date is approximately 30 days before the quarter-end due date (e.g., around March 1 for a March 31 due date). If a borrower has multiple contacts, confirm with the deal team who should receive the invoice. Keep a record of the send date for each borrower in case of payment disputes. |

### Step 5: Track Incoming Payments & Update Loan Servicing Model
| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Inputs** | Sent invoices (from Step 4) showing amounts due per borrower. Incoming payment notifications visible in Wells Fargo. |
| **Where inputs come from** | Wells Fargo Online Portal (View Only, individual credentials). Loan servicing model on SharePoint. |
| **Software & tools** | Wells Fargo Online Portal (View Only); Microsoft Excel (SharePoint — loan servicing model) |
| **Who is responsible** | Finance & Operations Associate |
| **Outputs** | Loan servicing model updated to reflect all payments received, including early payments noted with the actual receipt date. |
| **Where outputs go** | Updated loan servicing model saved to SharePoint. No external output required. |
| **Notes** | Monitor the Wells Fargo portal starting from the invoice send date. As payments come in: (1) Mark the payment as received on the official due date in the loan servicing model (this is standard practice). (2) If a borrower paid early, add a note directly to the Excel cell where the due date is recorded (e.g., "Paid early — received [actual date]"). This preserves the accurate due-date record while flagging the early receipt for future reference. Most reconciliation happens in the week after the quarter ends. If a payment has not arrived by the due date, flag it to the relevant deal team via Slack. |

## 8. Exception Handling

**Borrower with Capitalized or Waived Interest**
Do not exclude a borrower from invoicing without explicit written confirmation from the deal team via Slack. Note the reason for exclusion on the borrower list for that quarter.

**Principal Repayment or Fee Not in Loan Servicing Model**
Some invoices include amounts beyond regular interest. If the loan servicing model does not include a principal or fee amount, reach out to the deal team via Slack to confirm the correct figure before creating the invoice.

**Missing Borrower Contact Information**
Borrower contact details (name and email) are provided by the deal team at deal closing and should be on file. If the contact information is missing or outdated, request the correct details from the relevant deal team via Slack before sending the invoice.

**Payment Not Received by Due Date**
If a borrower's payment has not appeared in Wells Fargo by the due date, flag this to the deal team via Slack immediately. Do not take further action without deal team guidance — they manage the borrower relationship and will determine next steps.

**Borrower Disputes an Invoice Amount**
Direct any borrower disputes or questions to the deal team. The Finance & Operations team does not negotiate loan terms or payment amounts — that is the deal team's responsibility.
