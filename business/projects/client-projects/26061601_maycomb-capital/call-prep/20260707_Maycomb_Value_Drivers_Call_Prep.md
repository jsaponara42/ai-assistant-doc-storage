### 1. Value Drivers — the core of the call

Goal: identify the actions that actually drive revenue at Maycomb — deal sourcing, capital deployment speed, investor/impact reporting — since these aren't usually where people look for automation opportunities, and none of the discovery brief's six registered problems (P-001–P-006) currently touch this territory.

**Deal pipeline & capital deployment (Affinity / CRM)**
- Now that Affinity is replacing Salesforce, what does the deal pipeline actually look like stage to stage — sourcing, screening, diligence, term sheet, close?
- Where does time get lost between "deal sourced" and "capital deployed"? Is there a stage that consistently drags?
- What has to happen manually today to move a deal from one stage to the next in Affinity?

**Accelerator Fund intake triage**
- How is the new Accelerator Fund's any-format inbound (email, video, voice memo, "napkin") currently being triaged and organized?
- Who touches an inbound lead first, and what do they have to do to turn it into something the deal team can act on?
- Is anything getting lost or delayed because the intake formats are so inconsistent?

**Quarterly investor & impact reporting**
- How much of the quarterly investor and impact reporting is hand-built each cycle, post-AGM?
- Where does the underlying data come from — is it pulled from the loan servicing model, Affinity, LeverPoint financials, or assembled by hand from multiple sources?
- Is this the same bottleneck type as budget-to-actuals (expert judgment required), or more of a formatting/assembly grind?

**Outcome data (impact/mission-aligned lending)**
- How is outcome data currently collected and validated from service providers and government partners?
- Is this self-reported by borrowers, verified by the deal team, or some mix? How much manual chasing is involved?
- Does this data feed directly into the investor/impact reporting above, or is it a separate workstream?

**Open-ended close**
- Beyond what we've mapped so far, where do you personally feel like your time — or the deal team's time — goes toward something that doesn't need your judgment, but nobody's thought to automate because it's not a "back office" task?
