## Call-Specific Items Answered (Not Previously in Outstanding Questions)

These came out of the call and are new findings worth documenting here:

**AP Run Process — Key clarification on LeverPoint's role:**
LeverPoint does not receive invoices directly from vendors. Danielle receives every invoice, sanity-checks it, and forwards it to LeverPoint herself. LeverPoint then sets up payments on the bank account (Maycomb does not have direct bank access for cash control reasons). The current process involves a double-tracking system (Danielle's own tracker + LeverPoint's tracker) that was identified on the call as potentially redundant work. A **shared mailbox** for invoices was identified as the most impactful near-term process improvement — low-cost, no AI required, eliminates the reconciliation/duplication step.

**AP Run timing:**
- AP runs happen **every two weeks** (bi-weekly).
- Typical volume: **10–20 invoices per AP run.**
- Danielle's review time: approximately **10 minutes** to review the full list and allocations.
- Biggest bottleneck: **getting management approval** (Ariella/Miljana sign-off). Described as a *"constant nudging process"* — they're very busy and AP approval slips on their priority list.
- LeverPoint response time once approved: **a few hours.**
- Total elapsed time from first draft to payments released: **approximately 3 days** when things go well; **up to a week** when approvals lag.
- Payment portal approval (final release): another separate approval step that Danielle cannot perform herself (no authority); requires another round of nudging.

**Legal Bill Tracking — Three allocation categories confirmed:**
1. **Management Company:** Legal work related to internal policies, operational matters — easily and immediately allocable by Danielle. Essentially never requires escalation.
2. **Fund Responsibility:** Legal work tied to fund formation/docs (e.g., subscription docs, fund agreements) — also relatively clear, allocated to the specific fund (e.g., due-from-home Fund One). Paid by management company, then allocated/reimbursed quarterly by the fund. One exception noted: Fund Two has hit a legal fee cap in a specific category, so some Fund Two bills that would normally go to the fund must instead be allocated to the management company.
3. **Deal/Borrower Level:** Legal work tied to a specific loan or amendment (e.g., loan agreement, extension). The borrower is typically responsible for the legal bill, but Maycomb pays the legal vendor and waits to be reimbursed (either via invoice to borrower or withheld from a disbursement). Maycomb **does not pay the legal vendor until reimbursement is received.** This requires Danielle to track outstanding reimbursements and set reminders. Approximately **50% of deal-level allocation decisions come back requiring revision** after the deal team reviews — deal-specific context (e.g., borrower financial hardship) often overrides the initial recommendation.

**Legal Bill volume and time:**
- Legal bills arrive approximately **weekly.**
- Allocation for management company and fund bills: Danielle can determine immediately with high accuracy (essentially 0% revision rate).
- Deal-level allocation: requires deal team confirmation every time; ~50% revision rate.
- Key pain point: tracking whether the borrower has paid the reimbursement invoice, and nudging when they haven't.

**Quarterly Borrower Invoicing — Key process detail:**
- Approximately **15 active borrowers** currently receive quarterly invoices.
- Invoice amounts come from Danielle's **Excel loan servicing model** (accrued interest tab, per-borrower).
- Process: copy prior quarter's invoice, update dates and amount, send. Most time is spent pulling the right number from the model.
- Deal team check (confirming any fee adjustments, skipped quarters, quirks) happens **before** invoice drafts are created.
- Total time once deal team confirms: approximately **1 hour** for all 15 invoices.
- LeverPoint has their own deal tracking system but Maycomb does not rely on it for invoicing — built their own Excel model for visibility.

**Gripe list / roadmap:**
- **Home: Slack** (confirmed by Danielle).
- **Surfacing cadence: bi-weekly roadmap meeting** (confirmed by Danielle).
- Named Varenka as likely point person for this process going forward, with Miljana also mentioned as a potential participant.

**Claude Enterprise security configuration:**
- JC recommended **turning off Cowork and Claude Code by default** across the org, citing terminal access/cybersecurity risk.
- Barry affirmed this makes sense.
- No other Claude Enterprise admin items were raised or resolved on this call.
