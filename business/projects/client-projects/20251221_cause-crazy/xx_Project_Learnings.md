# Cause Crazy — Project Learnings

Closed engagement. Retroactive notes, captured 2026-07-11 while reflecting on how to run things differently going forward.

---

### Working model was too high-touch and too open-ended

The check-in cadence and board-tending took more of my own unstructured time than it should have, and the retainer had no clear time boundary, so scope crept without either of us really deciding that. The client also came to expect more availability and responsiveness than I'd actually intended to commit to. None of that was ever made explicit upfront, so there was nothing to point back to when it started to feel off.

---

### No proper MSA for the retainer conversion, and it caused real payment problems

Cause Crazy started as a $4,000 project-based fee. Once that was delivered, we upgraded to a $2,500/month retainer scoped for three months. I didn't have a proper MSA in place for that transition, and it caused real problems. Rocky Fischer (the client) was constantly anxious about whether he was "earning" his monthly retainer, even in months where the project stalled because of his own delays, not mine. His card also got declined after the first subscription payment, so we switched to direct invoicing, and after that he paid late and inconsistently every month. It came to a head when he tried to request a refund for the last month's retainer. The payment miscommunication, and how poorly he handled it, is the actual reason we didn't continue working together, not any disagreement about the work itself.

**Takeaway:** get a real MSA in place before converting a project fee into a retainer, especially around what "earning" a retainer means when delays are the client's fault, not mine. Lock in a reliable payment method before the first month, not after one already fails.
