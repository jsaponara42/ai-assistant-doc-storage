### Session Flow (shared shape, both days)

**1. Kickoff — 2 minutes**

Frame it simply: *"We're documenting how you actually do your job today — not how it should work, not the ideal version. That's what lets us find the real quick-win automations. There's no wrong answer here — if something's clunky or manual, say so, that's exactly what we want to hear. You'll be doing this on your own time afterward — today is just showing you how."*

**2. Explain the dictation process — the most important part of the call**

This is the core of the call. Explain the method so each person can go do it solo afterward, correctly, without JC in the room:

- **What "dictating" means here:** talking through their role and how they currently handle a given workflow, out loud, in as much real detail as they'd naturally describe it to a new hire — not a polished or idealized version.
- **The six things to cover** (give this as a checklist people can keep in mind while they talk, not a form to fill out line by line):
  - What do you need to start this? (**Inputs**)
  - Where does that come from? (**Source**)
  - What software/tools do you touch? (**Systems**)
  - Who else is involved, and what's their part? (**Owners**)
  - What comes out the other end? (**Outputs**)
  - Where does that output go — who or what system receives it? (**Hand-off**)
- **Why it matters:** this raw, out-loud description is the actual source material — it's what eventually gets written up as a clean SOP and is also what surfaces the real automation opportunities, so the more honest and specific the dictation, the more useful the result.
- **They'll be doing this repeatedly, on their own.** Make clear this isn't a one-time exercise — each person has multiple distinct workflows in their role, and they'll need to run this same process once per workflow, on their own time, not just once overall. Point them to the priority order in the day-specific section below as a starting sequence, not a complete list — they should keep going through their other workflows after the first one or two.
- **No live demo from JC.** JC explains the method but does not dictate his own workflow — nobody dictates anything live on this call.

**3. Claude — how they'll actually do it, shown once**

This is the only point where Claude itself comes up, and it's a quick show-and-tell, not a live working session. Show the group the prompt they'll paste into a Claude chat, on their own, each time they sit down to dictate a workflow:

> I'm going to describe my role and how I currently handle **[workflow name]** at Capital Financing. Ask me clarifying questions one at a time as I go if anything is unclear. When I say I'm done, write up everything I've described as a clean, numbered, step-by-step SOP that someone with no context could follow. Flag anything ambiguous or unconfirmed as `[TO CONFIRM]` rather than guessing.

Walk through what happens after they paste it: they talk, Claude asks follow-ups, they say "I'm done," Claude writes the SOP. That's the whole loop — repeated once per workflow, solo, whenever they have time.

**4. Claude Team setup — last, ~5 minutes, do not troubleshoot live**

Share the join link and have everyone click through and log in during the call, since they'll need this to actually go do the work afterward:


```
https://claude.ai/join/org/Ccg9eZuKOphA-dMY23rWKw
```

Confirm each person can see they're in the Capital Financing workspace — that's the only success check needed live. **If anything doesn't work (login issue, wrong email, etc.), don't stop to fix it in the group** — tell them to message JC directly afterward and it'll get sorted one-on-one. This matters more than usual here since a login problem now means they can't start their homework at all.

**5. Wrap-up — what to do with finished SOPs, and what happens next**

- Once a person finishes dictating a workflow and Claude produces the SOP write-up, they email the finished text to JC at **`[TO CONFIRM — JC's preferred email/address for this]`** — plain copy-paste into the email body is fine, no special formatting needed.
- No filename convention required on their end — JC will fold it into the vault.
- Remind them: one workflow per session is fine — this doesn't need to be done in one sitting, and they should expect to come back to it multiple times to cover everything in their role.
- Let the group know: once SOPs start coming in, JC may follow up individually if something needs more detail — a short one-on-one follow-up call is likely for at least one or two people, not a failure state, just normal next-pass clarification (same pattern as the `[TO CONFIRM]` items already in the existing workflow map).

### Thursday — Ops Team (Christy, Yasmine, Danielle)

**Why this order:** These three own three consecutive steps of the same pipeline — Underwrite → Agreement → Execute/Disburse. Christy carries the single biggest institutional-knowledge risk on the whole account, which is why her workflow should be the first one dictated regardless of pipeline order.

**1. Christy — dictate Underwriting Assessment (Tier 1 & Tier 2) first**

Why first: this is the most urgent documentation gap in the entire engagement. She's the sole holder of the underwriting heuristic — assessing whether attorney-provided case facts hold up, independent of what the firm claims — and it currently exists only in her head (PO-001, twenty-year tenure, no extraction). If she's out sick or leaves, this judgment leaves with her.

What her dictation should cover:

- How she vets attorney-provided facts — what she's actually checking for, not just "I don't take it at face value"
- Where Rayna/Leifert's Tier 1 training currently stands and what's still gated behind her

**2. Yasmine — dictate Contracting (Agreement Issuance & Execution) first**

Why this one first: picks up right where Christy's output ends (approved terms → agreement sent → executed), and this step has several open `[TO CONFIRM]` gaps in the existing workflow map — e-sign platform, exact system used, what triggers agreement issuance.

### Friday — BDR Team (Audrey, Brian, Victoria)

- Once she gets to it: how she currently handles the boundary between her side business and Capital Financing leads, since that's a live guardrail conversation with Howie right now — worth its own dictation
