# Diagnostic Register

## PO-001 — No Institutional Knowledge Capture Process

**Area:** Systems / Processes

**Phantom Output:** No assigned process for capturing institutional knowledge in a linkable digital source. Information lives in people's heads or siloed files — not accessible, not transferable, not scalable. Twenty-year operational tenure concentrated in Christy with no documented extraction.

**Phantom Filter:** The operation runs on people who know what to do. Writing it down is overhead — the team knows the process.

**Success Filter:** What lives in people's heads leaves with them. Documentation is not overhead — it is the infrastructure that makes the operation transferable, trainable, and scalable.

**SnapTrap Status:** Not started

## PO-002 — No HR Framework

**Area:** People

**Phantom Output:** No defined hiring criteria, performance standards, role baselines, or accountability structure. People decisions are made by feel. Two key hires (AI engineer, fractional CMO) in motion simultaneously into an unstructured People area.

**Phantom Filter:** Intuition is enough. A system feels unnecessary when your read of people has worked often enough. Hiring solves the gap — get the right people in and the problem corrects itself.

**Success Filter:** Intuition is a signal, not a standard. A framework does not replace your read — it protects the organization when your read isn't in the room. Hiring into a gap without a defined role, performance standard, and accountability structure installs a person into a system that will fail them.

**SnapTrap Status:** Not started

## PO-003 — AI Not Utilized at Operational Level

**Area:** Tools

**Phantom Output:** Active interest in AI with no implementation framework. Hiring an engineer before defining what AI is solving. The engineer hire without a defined use case or integration plan is an expensive experiment, not operational leverage.

**Phantom Filter:** Hiring an AI engineer solves the AI problem. Get the right person in and they will figure out what to do with it.

**Success Filter:** AI implementation without defined use cases and mapped workflows produces an experiment, not leverage. The engineer needs a blueprint, not a blank slate.

**SnapTrap Status:** Not started

## PO-004 — CRM Poorly Structured / Inconsistent Data Logging

**Area:** Systems / Tools

**Phantom Output:** Salesforce heavily built but largely unused by the sales team. No automation layer. Intake running through emailed templates instead of CRM. Reports built and never opened. Data logging inconsistent because there are no performance standards tied to what gets recorded.

**Phantom Filter:** The CRM is the problem. A better platform will produce better data.

**Success Filter:** The CRM reflects the standards the operation holds people to. No standards means no consistent logging — regardless of platform. Replacing the tool before fixing the standard replicates the dysfunction in a more expensive system.

**SnapTrap Status:** Not started

## PO-005 — Sales Time Not Productive

**Area:** People / Processes

**Phantom Output:** CEO is the number one salesperson, the default sales manager, the conference logistics operator, and the visionary — simultaneously. Sales consultants not following up on segmented post-conference contact lists. Team failing to convert warm leads handed to them by the CEO. Manual email cadence producing near-zero response. Sales side unmanaged and undisciplined.

**Phantom Filter:** More effort, more activity, more motion will produce more results. The CEO staying involved keeps the team on track.

**Success Filter:** Sales productivity is a systems problem, not an effort problem. Protected CEO time and a qualified, managed pipeline produce more than increased activity against an unqualified one. The CEO staying in the middle of sales execution is the constraint, not the solution.

**SnapTrap Status:** Not started

**Phantom Output:** Victoria — three years, commission-only since January 2025, South Florida only, does not follow direction, does not report commissions on time, does not develop business outside her state. Howie has mentally fired her but is avoiding the act because direct cost is low.

## Plan Overview

Eight Phantom Outputs diagnosed across four operational areas. The constraint cluster is People and Systems. Tools ambitions are running ahead of the foundation. Multiple high-investment vendor projects are converging without coordination architecture. The plan addresses the foundation first, then the growth moves Howie wants. Sequencing is non-negotiable. ## Area 1 — People

**Phantom Outputs in scope:** PO-002, PO-005, PO-006

**PO-002 — HR Framework**

SnapTrap: Install the HR framework as a decision support tool, not a replacement for judgment. Frame it as protection for Christy and the operation — not a constraint on Howie.

Sprint — Human Sprint: Define role baselines, performance standards, and accountability checkpoints for every current position. Christy leads execution. This Sprint is the prerequisite for both the AI engineer hire and the Margarita onboarding. Neither closes before it completes.

Key Result: Every active role has a written performance standard. Both pending hires onboard into defined roles with 30/60/90 day performance benchmarks from day one.

Timeline: Weeks 1–3.

**PO-005 — Sales Productivity and CEO Bandwidth**

SnapTrap: Diagnose where Howie's sales time is actually going. Identify the specific bleeds — operational involvement, unqualified conversations, no follow-up structure, no pipeline visibility. The solution is structural, not motivational.

Sprint — Process Sprint: Map Howie's current sales week. Eliminate the three largest time bleeds. Build a minimum viable pipeline process Christy can manage without Howie's involvement between touchpoints. Define the consultant accountability framework — activity minimums, reporting cadence, pipeline ownership. Victoria exit framed as a KPI-triggered system decision, not a personal call.

Key Result: Howie's net selling time increases by a defined number of protected hours per week. Pipeline has a visible stage structure. Consultants own their segments. CEO is out of daily sales management.

Timeline: Weeks 1–4 (2 weeks diagnostic, 2 weeks build). Runs concurrent with PO-002.

**PO-006 — Victoria Exit**

SnapTrap: The exit is not an emotional decision. It is a system decision. The KPI framework being built in PO-005 provides the objective trigger.

Sprint — Human Sprint: Once performance standards are defined, Victoria's record against them is documented. Exit is communicated as a KPI outcome, not a character judgment. Frame protects Howie and sets the standard for the team.

Key Result: Exit completed cleanly. Slot evaluated for reallocation or elimination based on sales structure output from PO-005 Sprint.

Timeline: Week 3–4, after KPI framework is in place.
