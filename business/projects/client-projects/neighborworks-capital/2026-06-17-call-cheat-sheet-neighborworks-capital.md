# Call Cheat Sheet — NeighborWorks Capital

**Companion to:** [[2026-06-17-call-brief-neighborworks-capital]]
**Goal of this call:** Win Tiana as champion. NOT to close. Neither attendee holds budget — success = Tiana leaves convinced you get her world and wants to bring you in front of whoever signs.

---

## The Room (read before you dial)

- **Tiana Coll** — Director, Analysis & Technology Operations. *Your champion.* Owns reporting, tech stack, vendor management, cybersecurity. 20 yrs in the field (15 at Enterprise Community Partners). Speaks data + infrastructure, not budget politics. She will instantly clock whether you actually get CDFI ops.
- **Rachel Haywood** — Office Manager. *Your ground-truth.* Feels the daily manual-admin pain firsthand. Use her for the unglamorous hour-eaters Tiana might abstract over.
- **Budget is NOT in the room.** New C-suite (CEO since 2022, 4 new C-suite since 2023) — an empowered, modernization-hungry leadership team sits above this call. Tiana carrying your name upward is the win.

---

## Open Fast (~3 min — protect your dig-deeper time)

1. **Why free** — say it before they ask. "I'm learning how these patterns show up across industries; in exchange you get a free roadmap and maybe a quick win."
2. **Why you** — domain fluency, not credentials. Don't recite their $600M / press releases back at them.
3. **Defuse the Salesforce threat early** — *"I'm not here to replace what you're building. I'm here to find the gaps Salesforce won't close on its own, and where automation could take load off your one project manager."*

> Lesson from Maycomb: you ran out of clock right as you reached your highest-value move. Open fast, dig in the middle, leave a clean final stretch.

---

## The Three Moments

### Confidence → name a pain she hasn't said yet
> *"In most CDFIs your size, assembling the portfolio report for the loan committee, the board, investors, AND CDFI Fund compliance is the same data pulled four different ways by hand every cycle. Is that you?"*

If you're right, you've proven you understand her world without a single credential. Worth more than any stat.

### Comfort → remove the catch + the threat
- Keep the "why free" framing (worked at Maycomb).
- Reframe automation as **giving her experts their time back**, not replacing anyone. A ~25-person shop has tenured people who hear "automation" as "layoffs."

### Excitement → provoke HER "imagine if"
Pick her most painful recurring report, walk it to the relief ending, let her finish the thought:
> *"So instead of your team hand-pulling borrower financials and rebuilding the committee report every cycle — the data comes in structured, the draft assembles itself, and you're reviewing instead of building."*

System-owners light up when they can see the architecture. (This is the Ariella variance-riff move.)

---

## Use Rachel deliberately

Don't let her be a bystander. Give her permission to complain (the Maycomb "pile it on" move):
> *"Rachel — what eats your day that shouldn't be a person's job?"*

Gets ground-truth AND shows the room you care about the doer, not just the decision-maker.

---

## The Budget-Path Move (near the end, after rapport)

Don't ask "who controls the budget?" — clumsy, signals selling. Instead:
> *"If we found something here worth building, who else would want to be part of that conversation?"*

Surfaces the approval path AND recruits Tiana as your advocate upward without making her feel routed around.

---

## Five Sharpest Questions

1. "Walk me through how a portfolio or investor report comes together today — who touches it, and how many hours does one cycle take?"
2. "When a borrower's financials are due, how does that follow-up actually happen — and how often does missing data hold up reporting?"
3. "Where are you in the Salesforce expansion, and what's the gap between what you want it to do and what it does today?"
4. "What have you already tried to fix the manual reporting side, and where did it fall short?"
5. "Rachel — what eats the most of your day that feels like it shouldn't be a person's job?"

---

## Red Flags to Hold in Mind

- Budget authority not in the room → don't scope paid work today; aim for the next conversation with the right people.
- "We just hired people / bought Salesforce to handle this" → they may feel it's already being solved. Position as reinforcing, not competing.
- CDFI on grants + investor capital → procurement is slow and cautious. Expect a longer path than a for-profit of the same size.
