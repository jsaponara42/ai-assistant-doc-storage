# Pre-Call Screening Survey
**Blue Tusk LLC · Free Workflow Waste Audit · Calendly Attachment**

---

## Instructions for Setup - test

Attach this as a Calendly intake form on your audit booking page. Keep it short — the goal is to qualify intent, not interrogate them. Most firms should pass. The only real disqualifier is someone who won't invest in fixing the problems the audit surfaces.

Recommended Calendly field types are noted in brackets.

**Calendly event:** https://calendly.com/bluetuskllc/blue-tusk-workflow-audit

---

## Survey Questions

**1. What is your firm's name?**
[Short text] — Required

---

**2. What is your role at the firm?**
[Dropdown] — Required
- Managing Attorney / Owner
- Partner
- Office Manager
- Other

---

**3. Approximately how many leads or new case inquiries does your firm receive per month?**
[Dropdown] — Required
- Fewer than 20
- 20–50
- 51–100
- 101–250
- 250+

*Internal note: Fewer than 20 is a soft flag — not a hard disqualifier, but worth noting. Volume this low may limit how quickly automations produce visible ROI. Proceed but set expectations accordingly in the audit.*

---

**4. How many people work at your firm, including yourself?**
[Dropdown] — Required
- Just me
- 2–5
- 6–15
- 16–50
- 50+

---

**5. Which of the following best describes your current intake and case management process?**
[Dropdown] — Required
- Fully manual — we do most things by hand or phone
- Mostly manual with a few tools (CRM, spreadsheet, etc.)
- Mix of manual and automated — some things are systematized, some aren't
- Mostly automated — we have solid systems in place

*Internal note: "Mostly automated" is a soft flag. Worth a quick conversation, but the audit may surface less low-hanging fruit. Proceed — there's still usually something.*

---

**6. What is the biggest operational challenge your firm is facing right now?**
[Long text — open ended] — Optional

*Internal note: Read this before the call. This is their stated burning problem. Reference it early in Phase 4 of the call to show you actually read their answers.*

---

**7. If it became clear during the audit that automating parts of your workflow could save you significant time and money, are you in a position to act on that within the next 90 days?**
[Dropdown] — Required
- Yes — if the ROI is there, I'm ready to move
- Probably — I'd want to see the numbers first
- Not right now — I'm just exploring for now
- No — I don't have budget or bandwidth at the moment

*Internal note: "No" is the primary disqualifier. If someone selects this, you can still run the audit — but flag internally that conversion to a retainer is unlikely in the near term. "Not right now — just exploring" is not a disqualifier; many people who say this convert after seeing their dollar figures in writing.*

---

**8. Which tools are you currently using? (Select all that apply)**
[Multi-select checkboxes] — Optional
- Clio
- Lawmatics
- Filevine
- MyCase
- Salesforce
- HubSpot
- Calendly or other scheduling tool
- DocuSign or other e-signature tool
- Spreadsheets only
- None / not sure
- Other (please specify)

*Internal note: Review before the call. Knowing their stack lets you skip the tools questions in Phase 2 and go deeper.*

## Disqualification Logic Summary

| Answer | Action |
|---|---|
| Q7: "No — I don't have budget or bandwidth" | Soft disqualify. Can still run audit, but deprioritize follow-up effort. Consider a nurture sequence instead. |
| Q3: Fewer than 20 leads/month | Proceed but note low volume in report. Temper ROI estimates. |
| Q5: Mostly automated | Proceed. Audit may surface metrics/dashboard gap even if quick wins are minimal. |
| All other combinations | Fully qualified. Proceed normally. |
