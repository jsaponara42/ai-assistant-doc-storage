**Date:** 2026-06-16
**Industry:** Impact investing / private credit (fund manager)

---

## The Company
- **What they do:** Impact investing platform providing mission-aligned debt and equity to social enterprises. Flagship is the Community Outcomes Fund — the largest US fund dedicated to outcomes / pay-for-success financing — plus a custom strategies arm and the Social Entrepreneurs Fund (TSEF).
- **Size:** ~16–20 person team, Brooklyn HQ (Denver presence). ~$140M AUM as of 2023.
- **Stage:** Established & growing — founded 2016, actively hiring (Investment Associate + Analyst), launching new vehicles (Outcomes Finance Accelerator Fund w/ ZOMA).
- **Tech signals:** Gatsby site w/ DatoCMS, web contact form, newsletter capture. No visible CRM, deal-flow, or fund-admin tooling. New-fund outreach accepts "email, video, voice memo, or a napkin" — signals manual, unstructured intake.
- **Notable:** Women-owned. ImpactAssets IA50. Just held first Annual General Meeting (60+ investors). Heavy outcomes-reporting burden inherent to pay-for-success deals.

## Anticipated Pains
1. **Outcomes / impact reporting is manual.** Every pay-for-success deal hinges on collecting, validating, and reporting outcome data from service providers and governments — likely spreadsheets + email per transaction.
2. **Investor / LP reporting & comms.** First AGM just held (60+ investors). Quarterly LP updates, capital calls, bespoke impact narratives are time-intensive and recurring for a 5-person finance team.
3. **Unstructured deal intake.** New-fund applications arrive via "email, video, voice memo, or napkin." No structured pipeline = manual triage, data entry, inconsistent capture.
4. **Loan monitoring & portfolio admin.** Private-credit servicing — covenant tracking, borrower financials, disbursement schedules — recurring back-office load that grows per deal.
5. **Knowledge / diligence synthesis.** Reading lengthy program evaluations, government contracts, and evidence bases to judge "what works" — research-heavy, AI-summarizable.

---

## Sharp Questions to Ask
- "Walk me through what happens from a new outcomes deal coming in to the first investor report going out — where does your team spend the most hours?"
- "How are you collecting and validating outcome data from your service providers and government partners today?"
- "After the AGM — how much of the quarterly LP and impact reporting is hand-built each cycle?"
- "With the new Accelerator Fund taking applications in any format — how are you triaging and organizing that inbound right now?"
- "What's taking the most time in your operation right now that you wish wasn't?"
- "As you add headcount and deals, what breaks first in your current setup?"

---

## What to Listen For
A strong fit sounds like Ariella naming a specific recurring workflow that eats her small team's time — outcome data wrangling, LP reporting, or intake triage — and Danielle nodding because she's the one doing it. Listen for "we do this every quarter by hand," "it doesn't scale," or anxiety tied to the new fund + new hires. Both decision-maker (Ariella, budget) and implementer (Danielle) on the call is itself a green light to move toward a discovery session.

---

## Red Flags
Mission-driven, lean teams can treat manual process as a virtue ("the napkin" framing) rather than a cost — watch for resistance to automating high-touch, relationship-based work. Data sensitivity (government, beneficiary, financial) may raise legitimate AI-governance hesitancy. As a regulated fund manager, anything touching investment decisions or compliance will move slowly — anchor on back-office time savings, not deal judgment.
