## Summary
Four LinkedIn post concepts covering local AI computing, unified agent systems, machine readability, and meeting-to-action workflows.

## Content

### Post 1: The Local AI Reality Check
Running AI locally is getting better, but the math is still compelling for most people. A computer capable of running local models well costs significant money upfront. Compare that to per-token costs of cloud services or a monthly subscription — for the vast majority of people, cloud services are better, more capable, and more user friendly. For those with patience and a knack for tinkering, there's some local AI work you may enjoy doing. But it's a hobby, not a replacement.

### Post 2: The End of Individual Automations
Building individual automations one by one is fading. The next era is unified systems — an agent operating system where context flows between tools. The most important thing is getting all your project data, people, and information into a machine-readable format that an agent can access. Without that unified context layer, you're just building more silos.

### Post 3: Machine Readability Is Non-Negotiable
Companies need to make machine readability a priority. If your tools, data, and workflows aren't AI-readable through APIs, command-line interfaces, or proper tooling, you're going to get left behind. This isn't optional anymore — it's infrastructure. Make your outputs machine-readable or accept that you'll always be one step behind.

### Post 4: From Meeting Transcription to Real Action
Meeting transcriptions are usually dead ends. But with the right file setup, you can go directly from an AI meeting transcription to taking real action on a project. Structure your meetings, structure your outputs, and let AI bridge the gap between discussion and execution.
