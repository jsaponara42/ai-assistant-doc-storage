## Summary

Reference map of human needs from The Ellipsis Manual. Each need has an associated fear (what drives the need) and a weakness (how the need creates an opening for influence). Use this alongside the Needs Map checklist in the daily template.

---

## Needs map — types

| Need | Type | How it shows |
|---|---|---|
| Appreciation | Primary | Behaviors that benefit others or improve their environment; seeking to feel noticed for it — often without awareness |
| Acceptance | Primary | Willing to change behavior, speech, mannerisms, dress to fit a group or culture |
| Approval | Primary | Changes position when someone disagrees; asks permission unnecessarily; coaxes compliments from others |
| Intelligence | Secondary | References education, facts, academic achievements; needs to be seen as knowledgeable |
| Pity | Secondary | Surfaces pitfalls, tragedies, and misfortunes in conversation; seeking sympathy and support |
| Admiration | Secondary | Showcases property, achievements, life circumstances; harvesting envy |
| Power | Secondary | Doesn't need power — needs to FEEL powerful and for others to SEE them as such |

## How to use this in practice

**Identifying the need:** Listen for what someone talks about most, seeks validation on, or gets defensive about. Most needs surface within 2 minutes of real conversation.

**The fear is the lever:** Once you know the need, you know the fear. The fear is what makes the need a vulnerability — it's the direction from which pressure works.

**The weakness is the opening:** The weakness column tells you what kind of response from you will create movement. For example:
- Appreciation need → withdrawal of warmth creates urgency; genuine acknowledgment creates compliance
- Approval need → a subtle expression of disagreement followed by an approval window creates a change opportunity
- Intelligence need → genuine intellectual validation lowers guard significantly
- Comfort need → frame your ask as the low-disruption path; frame inaction as the disruptive choice
- Pity need → once you confirm and validate the pity narrative, compliance follows naturally

**Pairing with language seeding:** Certain needs map directly to the "letting go / open / appreciative" language tier:
- Appreciation → "appreciative" language feeds directly into their primary driver
- Acceptance / Approval → "open" language mirrors what they want to feel seen as
- Comfort → "letting go" language reduces perceived friction of change
