## Summary
Notes on techniques for building genuine human connection quickly — covering name retention, active listening, emotional attunement, and identifying underlying needs.

## Context
Captured as raw bullet notes on 2026-04-24 during personal research or reflection on interpersonal skills.

## Content

### Learning and Remembering Names
- Listen — actually listen when introduced
- Repeat the name at least twice: *"Chris, nice to meet you, Chris"*
- Ask how to spell it; pay a compliment while using the name; connect it to someone else you know
- Complete the full sequence in under 10 seconds

### Finding the Emotion Behind the Response
- When someone gives a surface-level answer, ask follow-up questions to uncover the emotion underneath
- Once the emotion is identified, relate it to something you've personally experienced

### Needs Mapping
- Use the needs map (ellipsis) to identify and understand the deeper needs driving a person's words or behavior
