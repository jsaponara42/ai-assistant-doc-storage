## Summary

Voice note capture about a psychological influence framework from *The Ellipsis Manual* by Chase Hughes. Two core levers: **authority** (credibility/positioning) and **novelty** (pattern interrupts that break autopilot). Applied to sales conversations and cold outreach contexts.

## Context

Captured as a voice note while thinking through influence techniques that could strengthen Blue Tusk sales calls and outreach. The book is framed around social engineering but the concepts map directly to persuasion in B2B sales contexts.

## Content

Two conditions must be present to guide someone effectively:

- **Authority** — the person must perceive you as credible and trustworthy enough to follow
- **Novelty** — a pattern break that disrupts their autopilot state, creating an opening for influence

Without novelty, the person remains in a defensive, habituated mode. Without authority, the pattern break doesn't convert into directed action.

**Potential applications:**
- Cold email subject lines / openers as pattern interrupts
- Discovery call openings to break "another vendor call" framing
- Objection handling pivots
