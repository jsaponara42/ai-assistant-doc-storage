## Content

### Realistic Timeline

- Gaining 20 lbs of muscle naturally takes **at least 1 year** with proper training and nutrition.
- Each subsequent year, gains slow by roughly half.
- Fastest gains come not when you *start* lifting, but when you *start lifting properly*.

---

### Training

#### Volume: How many sets per week?

- Top natural bodybuilders average ~**12 sets per muscle per week** (some as low as 6).
- More sets yield diminishing returns: sets 1–5 produce the most growth; beyond 10 sets/session, extra sets show no clear additional benefit.
- **Cap per session:** ~10–11 sets per muscle group per session; split remaining volume across 2+ days/week for up to 30% more gains.

#### Intensity: How close to failure?

- Training 8 reps short of failure = some growth.
- Training **1–2 reps short of failure = nearly double the growth**.
- Signs you're not training hard enough:
  - Last rep isn't moving really slowly.
  - You can do more reps on the last set than the first (same weight).

#### Two Main Approaches

| Method | Sets/Muscle/Week | Proximity to Failure | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Intensity Method** | 5–12 | To absolute failure | Shorter workouts (~20 min chest); brutal mentally |
| **Volume Method** | 12–20 | Stop 2–3 reps short | Longer workouts; easier mentally |

- Difference in outcomes is small — choose whichever you'll **stick to**.
- Mixing both is fine (e.g., arms/back → intensity; legs → volume).

#### Recommended Splits

Upper/lower, push/pull/legs, or full body — the split matters less than **consistency**.

### Exercise Selection (3 Stages)

**Stage 1 – Beginner:** Master 6 core movement patterns (press, pull, squat, hip hinge + 2 more). Done 3x/week, this alone can build 10+ lbs of muscle.

**Stage 2 – Intermediate (2–3 years in):** Compound lifts have limits for your body type. Experiment with machines, cables, and specialized exercises. Become your own guinea pig — find what gives the best pump, soreness, and feels good on joints.

**Stage 3 – Advanced:** Stop rotating exercises constantly. Double down on the ones that work; rotate only when needed.

**Expert exercise picks (Steve Hall, pro natural bodybuilder):**
- Delts: cross-body cable lateral raise
- Triceps: dumbbell skull crushers
- Biceps: Bayesian curls
- Chest: converging machine press
- Lats: shoulder-width lat pulldown
- Upper back: pronated grip machine row
- Glutes: machine hip thrust
- Quads: Cybex hack squat
- Hamstrings: RDL
- Calves: straight leg calf raise

### Nutrition

#### How Much to Eat (by body fat level)

| Body Fat | Approach | Target |
|---|---|---|
| >20% (men) / >30% (women) | Body recomposition | Deficit of max 250–500 cal/day; lose 0.5% bodyweight/week |
| Lean enough | Slight surplus | Aim for 0.5–2% bodyweight gain/month depending on experience |

**Calorie surplus targets by experience:**
- Beginner: ~2% body mass gain/month
- Intermediate: ~1%
- Advanced: ~0.5%

#### Protein

- Minimum to maximize muscle: **~1.6 g/kg bodyweight** (~0.7 g/lb)
- Still building muscle below that threshold (e.g., 1.2 g/kg)
- Protein has a smaller effect than commonly believed — don't obsess over it.

#### Pre-Workout Nutrition

- 1.5–2 hours before: slow-digesting carbs + protein (e.g., oats, Greek yogurt, protein powder)
- 30 minutes before: fast-digesting carbs for immediate energy

#### Supplements

- **Creatine**: Most evidence-backed. Adds 2–3 lbs lean mass in first 8–12 weeks (mostly water in muscles). ~20–30% of people are non-responders.
- No other supplements are necessary to build 20 lbs of muscle.

### Sleep & Recovery

- **7–9 hours/night** — but quality matters as much as quantity.
- Body produces more growth hormone and testosterone with better sleep; poor sleep = less recovery and limited gains.

**3 ways people sabotage sleep quality:**
1. **Light:** Room must be completely dark — even LED charger lights are too much. Use a blackout eye mask.
2. **Noise:** Quiet environment; earplugs recommended.
3. **Temperature:** Room should be cool; program thermostat to drop even lower by 2–4 AM.

**Naps:** A 20-minute nap can recover 2–3% of athletic performance lost from sleep deprivation — significant at competitive levels.

---

### Key Takeaways

1. Train hard (close to failure), train consistently, avoid injury.
2. Split volume across 2+ days/week; don't exceed ~10–11 sets/muscle/session.
3. Find exercises that suit *your* body — experiment, then commit.
4. Eat at the right surplus/deficit for your body fat level; protein matters less than most think.
5. Sleep is non-negotiable for growth hormone, recovery, and performance.
6. Consistency over perfection — nothing here is magic, but it works.
