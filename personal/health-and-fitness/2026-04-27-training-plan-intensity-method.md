## Summary

A 3-day beginner training plan using the Intensity Method: low weekly sets (5–8 per muscle group) taken to absolute failure. Designed for Planet Fitness access (~3 hrs/week gym time) plus one optional home bodyweight session. Built for a very lean beginner focused on maximizing muscle growth from scratch.

## Context

Based on the science-backed muscle building research note [[2026-04-27-muscle-building-science-backed]]. Intensity Method: fewer sets (5–8/muscle/week), every set pushed to complete failure — the point where you physically cannot move the weight another inch. As a beginner, the six core movement patterns (push, pull, hinge, squat, carry, rotation) are the focus. Compound lifts 3x/week with muscle groups hit at least 2x/week.

## Content

### How to Use This Plan

- **Gym days:** 3x/week — aim for Mon / Wed / Fri or Tue / Thu / Sat (at least one rest day between sessions)
- **Home day:** Once a week on a non-gym day (optional but recommended)
- **Session length:** 45–60 minutes in the gym
- **Failure means:** Your last rep is moving extremely slowly and you physically cannot complete another rep with good form. Not "this is hard" — actual failure.
- **Rest between sets:** 2–3 minutes (intensity method requires full recovery between sets)
- **Progression:** Every week, try to add 1 rep or 2.5–5 lbs to each exercise. If you can't, that's fine — just match last session.

---

### Weekly Structure

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Monday | Gym – Full Body A |
| Tuesday | Home – Bodyweight |
| Wednesday | Gym – Full Body B |
| Thursday | Rest |
| Friday | Gym – Full Body A |
| Saturday | Rest |
| Sunday | Rest |

*Alternate A and B each gym week. So next week: B / Home / A / Rest / B / Rest / Rest*

---

### Gym – Full Body A

**Target:** ~45–55 min

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Barbell or Smith Machine Squat** | 2 | 6–10 to failure | Go to complete failure. Rack safely. |
| 2 | **Seated Cable Row** (shoulder-width grip) | 2 | 8–12 to failure | Drive elbows back, squeeze. |
| 3 | **Dumbbell Bench Press** | 2 | 8–12 to failure | Full range of motion. |
| 4 | **Romanian Deadlift (RDL)** | 2 | 8–12 to failure | Hinge from hips, slight knee bend, bar close to legs. |

**Total sets:** 8 sets | **Muscles:** Quads, glutes, lats, chest, hamstrings

> **Rest 2–3 min between every set.** With that rest time, 8 sets takes ~35–40 min, leaving room for warm-up.

---

### Gym – Full Body B

**Target:** ~45–55 min

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Leg Press** | 2 | 8–12 to failure | Feet shoulder-width, full range. |
| 2 | **Lat Pulldown** (shoulder-width, pronated grip) | 2 | 8–12 to failure | Pull to upper chest, control the return. |
| 3 | **Dumbbell Shoulder Press** | 2 | 8–12 to failure | Seated or standing. Full extension at top. |
| 4 | **Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift** | 2 | 8–12 to failure | Same hinge pattern as A, now with dumbbells. |

**Total sets:** 8 sets | **Muscles:** Quads, glutes, lats, delts, hamstrings

---

### Home – Bodyweight Session

**Target:** 20–30 min | No equipment needed

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Push-ups** | 2 | To failure | Slow down the last 3 reps. Elevate feet for more chest/shoulder emphasis once easy. |
| 2 | **Bodyweight Squat or Jump Squat** | 2 | To failure | Jump squat for more intensity. Land soft. |
| 3 | **Superman Hold** | 2 | 10–15 slow reps | Lie face down, lift chest + legs simultaneously. Hold 2 sec at top. |
| 4 | **Pike Push-up** | 1 | To failure | Targets shoulders. Hips high, head toward floor. |

**Total sets:** 7 sets | **Purpose:** Active recovery + light stimulus; not a primary growth driver

### Exercise Notes for Planet Fitness

- **Smith Machine:** Use for squats if free barbells aren't available or feel unstable as a beginner.
- **Cable machines:** Nearly every PF has a full cable stack — use for rows and pulldowns.
- **Dumbbells:** PF typically goes up to 75–80 lbs. More than enough as a beginner.
- **No deadlift platform?** RDLs from the floor or a rack works fine. The trap bar (hex bar) is a great swap if available.
- **Lunk Alarm:** Controlled, slow failure reps won't trigger it. No dropping weights.

### Progression Roadmap

**Months 1–2 (Beginner Phase):**
- Focus on learning the movement patterns. Perfect form beats heavy weight every time.
- Failure at this stage might feel like "burning" or "shaking" — that's right.
- Expect to add reps or small weight every 1–2 sessions (newbie gains are real).

**Months 3–6:**
- Progression slows. That's normal.
- If you stall on a lift for 2+ weeks, try adding one set before adding weight.
- Begin noticing which exercises feel best for your body.

**Month 6+ (Reassess):**
- Review the science-backed note on Stage 2 exercise selection.
- Begin experimenting with machine/cable variants for lagging muscles.
- Consider adding 1–2 isolation movements (e.g., bicep curls, calf raises).
