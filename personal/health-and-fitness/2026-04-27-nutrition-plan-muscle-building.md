## Summary

A calorie-surplus nutrition plan tailored for a very lean beginner (5'7", 125 lbs, hard gainer) focused on maximizing muscle growth. Based on science-backed targets: ~2% bodyweight gain/month, 1.6 g protein/kg, and a meaningful calorie surplus. Includes daily calorie targets, macro breakdown, meal timing, and supplement guidance.

Related training plan: [[2026-04-27-training-plan-intensity-method]]

## Context

Based on the science-backed muscle building research note [[2026-04-27-muscle-building-science-backed]]. At >20% body fat, a recomposition (deficit) approach works. But at 5'7" / 125 lbs, body fat is very low — this profile benefits most from a **calorie surplus** to fuel muscle growth. As a beginner, the body is highly responsive to training stimulus, so gains come faster now than at any future point. Don't waste the window.

### Macro Targets

| Macro | Daily Target | Calories |
|---|---|---|
| **Protein** | 95 g | ~380 cal |
| **Carbohydrates** | ~330 g | ~1,320 cal |
| **Fat** | ~90 g | ~810 cal |
| **Total** | — | **~2,510 cal** |

**Why this split:**
- Protein is set at the minimum-for-maximum threshold (1.6 g/kg). You don't need to go higher — it won't meaningfully speed up gains and adds cost.
- Carbs are high intentionally — as a hard gainer, carbs are your friend. They fuel workouts, replenish muscle glycogen, and make it easier to hit a calorie surplus.
- Fat is moderate — supports hormones (including testosterone, which matters for muscle growth).

### Meal Timing

Timing matters most around workouts. Outside of that, just hit your daily totals.

#### On Gym Days

| Time | Meal | What to Eat |
|---|---|---|
| ~1.5–2 hrs before training | **Pre-workout meal** | Slow-digesting carbs + protein (see examples below) |
| ~30 min before training | **Pre-workout snack** | Fast-digesting carbs (fruit, white rice cake, sports drink) |
| Within 1–2 hrs after training | **Post-workout meal** | Protein + carbs (most important meal of the day) |
| Rest of day | 2–3 more meals | Spread remaining calories and protein across the day |

#### On Rest/Home Days

- No special timing needed. Just hit your daily calorie and protein targets across 3–4 meals.

### Sample Daily Meal Plan (~2,500 cal)

#### Meal 1 – Breakfast (Pre-workout if training in the morning)
- 1 cup oats (dry) — 300 cal, 10g protein, 54g carbs
- 1 cup Greek yogurt (plain, full fat) — 150 cal, 15g protein
- 1 scoop protein powder (mixed in or on the side) — 120 cal, 25g protein
- 1 banana — 100 cal, 27g carbs
- **Meal total: ~670 cal | ~50g protein**

#### Meal 2 – Lunch
- 2 cups cooked white rice — 400 cal, 88g carbs
- 4 oz chicken breast or canned tuna — 180 cal, 35g protein
- 1 tbsp olive oil (drizzled or for cooking) — 120 cal, 14g fat
- Mixed vegetables (any) — 50 cal
- **Meal total: ~750 cal | ~37g protein**

#### Meal 3 – Dinner
- 4 oz 90/10 ground beef or salmon — 220 cal, 28g protein
- 1.5 cups cooked pasta or rice — 330 cal, 68g carbs
- 1 tbsp butter or olive oil — 100 cal
- Vegetables — 50 cal
- **Meal total: ~700 cal | ~30g protein**

#### Snack – Anytime
- 2 tbsp peanut butter — 190 cal, 7g protein, 16g fat
- 2 slices whole wheat bread or crackers — 160 cal
- 1 cup whole milk — 150 cal, 8g protein
- **Snack total: ~500 cal | ~15g protein**

**Day total: ~2,620 cal | ~132g protein** ✓

> This is one example. Adjust portions up/down to hit your targets. The specific foods don't matter — the calories and protein do.

### Hard Gainer Strategies

If hitting 2,500+ calories feels difficult or you're still not gaining weight:

1. **Liquid calories are your friend.** A glass of whole milk, a smoothie, or a mass gainer shake is much easier to consume than a full meal. A cup of whole milk with each meal adds ~450 cal/day painlessly.

2. **Calorie-dense foods over high-volume foods.** Nuts, nut butters, olive oil, avocado, whole milk, dried fruit, and dark chocolate are all calorie-dense. Salads and vegetables are not.

3. **Don't skip breakfast.** Hard gainers often undereat in the morning. Make your first meal large.

4. **Eat on a schedule.** Hunger cues are unreliable for hard gainers. Set an alarm to eat every 3–4 hours whether you feel hungry or not.

5. **Track for at least 2–4 weeks.** You are almost certainly eating less than you think. Use Cronometer or MyFitnessPal to verify your actual intake before assuming you're eating enough.

### Supplements

Only two are worth your money as a beginner:

| Supplement | Dose | When | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Creatine Monohydrate** | 5g/day | Any time (consistency matters, not timing) | Proven to add 2–3 lbs lean mass in first 8–12 weeks; boosts strength output. Buy unflavored powder — cheapest and most effective form. |
| **Protein Powder** | As needed to hit 95g/day | Any meal or post-workout | Only useful if you're struggling to hit protein from food. Whey is cheapest and fast-absorbing. |

> Everything else (pre-workout, BCAAs, mass gainers, testosterone boosters) is either redundant, ineffective, or overpriced for a beginner. Save your money.

**On creatine non-response:** ~20–30% of people don't respond. After 6–8 weeks, if your strength/reps haven't increased at all, you may be a non-responder. It's fine to stop.

---

### Sleep (Non-Negotiable)

From the research: sleep is when growth hormone and testosterone spike. Poor sleep directly limits how much muscle you build regardless of training or diet.

- **Target:** 7–9 hours/night
- **Room:** Completely dark (eye mask if needed), quiet (earplugs), cold (65–68°F / 18–20°C)
- **Naps:** A 20-minute nap can recover meaningful performance lost from a bad night

### Tracking Your Progress

| Metric | How Often | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Bodyweight | Every morning (after bathroom, before eating) | +2–2.5 lbs/month |
| Calories | Daily for first 4 weeks, then weekly spot checks | 2,500–2,700/day |
| Protein | Daily for first 4 weeks | 90–100g/day |
| Gym lifts | Every session | Beat last session by 1 rep or 2.5–5 lbs |

> **Weight fluctuates daily by 1–3 lbs** due to water, food, and sodium. Judge progress by the weekly average, not any single weigh-in.
